## §1 First Branch — "The Rude Box" (Swamp Mimic)

**MAB:** You smell it before you see it. The trail ends in black water. Cypress knees jut from the muck like knuckles.
**MAB:** Somewhere ahead, every frog stops singing at once.
**BRONT:** I don't like quiet.
**SABLE:** I love quiet. Quiet is where the money is.
**MAB:** A boardwalk sags across the bog. Half the planks are gone. On a dry hummock in the middle sits a chest — iron-bound, waiting.
**SABLE:** Oh, hello, gorgeous.
**QUILL:** Sable, wait. Nothing dry survives out here — that's statistically impossible.
**FIFE:** And yet the heart wants what it wants! Onward, to riches!
**MAB:** Give me a check to pop the lock, Sable. `[d20 → 1]`
**SABLE:** ...it's a one, isn't it.
**MAB:** The lid yawns open on its own. Rows of wet teeth. The chest lunges and clamps down on your arm. Swamp Mimic — roll for initiative.
**QUILL:** I knew it! Mimic: 39 HP, resistant to acid, and its grab is a DC 13 to escape!
**BRONT:** Big box. Small problem. `[d20 → 13 hit, d8 → 1]` I hit it. Barely.
**FIFE:** Fear not — Fife the Undaunted strikes! `[d20 → 5 miss]` My rapier finds a plank. The plank is defeated.
**QUILL:** Hopeless. Firebolt — `[d20 → 9 miss]` and it hisses wide into the water.
**SABLE:** Fine. Nobody move. `[d20 → 20]`
**MAB:** Natural twenty. You find the seam where the lid meets the jaw and drive the dagger in to the hilt.
**QUILL:** Even a low crit is 2d6 doubled + your DEX — that's 8 damage!
**MAB:** The Mimic bites down harder. `[d20 → 20]` Natural twenty — it crits you back, Sable.
**SABLE:** Of course it does. Why would today be easy?
**BRONT:** Hold on. I've got you.
**MAB:** Bront heaves the thing off. It comes apart into splinters and gold. You're 6 gold richer, one arm ruined, and the frogs still won't sing.
**FIFE:** A glorious victory! I shall call it the Ballad of the Rude Box!

## §2 Waypoint — "The Bargain" (Green Hag)

**MAB:** Past the boardwalk, the water gives way to a slimed stone shrine, half-sunk, older than the swamp itself. A woman sits on the altar. Her smile has too many teeth.
**HAG:** Travelers! You look lost. And wet. I can fix one of those.
**QUILL:** Green Hag. 52 HP, casts illusions, and she never offers a fair deal — the manual is very clear on this.
**HAG:** Rude. I only want a small thing. A name. Give me a name you love, and I'll show you the dry path across.
**SABLE:** How small a name? Asking for a friend.
**BRONT:** Don't. Names are not for sale.
**MAB:** Roll to see through her illusion, Quill. `[d20 → 6]`
**QUILL:** Six. Ugh. The shrine looks lovely now. Suspiciously lovely.
**FIFE:** She's charming, I'll give her that. Fife almost agreed! `[d20 → 11]`
**MAB:** The path she shows you shimmers. Bront tests it with a boot. `[d20 → 10]`
**BRONT:** Solid for one step. Then not.
**HAG:** Last chance, sweetlings. A name, or a fight.
**SABLE:** We'll take the fight. `[d20 → 1]`
**MAB:** Natural one. You lunge, slip on the wet stone, and go down hard in front of her. She laughs — that costs you 5 HP and your dignity.
**QUILL:** This is why we plan things!
**BRONT:** Up you get. `[d20 → 10]` I put myself between you and the teeth.
**FIFE:** And now — a distraction worthy of song! `[d20 → 8]` I sing. It is not my best work.
**MAB:** But it's enough. The Hag's illusion cracks; the shrine is a ruin again, and she's gone, hissing, into the reeds. She leaves a bag: 14 gold and a small silver key.
**SABLE:** See? Kept my name. Got a key. Everyone wins.

## §3 Second Branch — "The Dead Don't Drown" (Mummy & Wight)

**MAB:** The silver key fits a lockbox in the mud — and the lockbox is a coffin lid. Two shapes sit up in the water. One wrapped in rotted linen. One in rusted mail.
**QUILL:** Oh, wonderful. A Mummy — 47 HP — and a Wight — 45 HP. The Mummy's touch rots you; save each turn or your max HP drops!
**BRONT:** Two of them. One of me. `[d20 → 4]` Missed. The mud is slow.
**SABLE:** Not for me. `[d20 → 14]` Dagger, meet spine. `[d6 → 5]`
**MAB:** The Wight staggers but keeps coming — the dead don't feel it the way the living do.
**FIFE:** Then we make them feel the MUSIC! `[d20 → 12]` A chord of pure defiance!
**QUILL:** Firebolt, this time for real. `[d20 → 12 hit, d8 → 4]` Linen burns beautifully.
**MAB:** The Mummy reaches for Quill. Save against the rot. `[d20 → 1]`
**QUILL:** A one. Of course a one. I can feel my own footnotes forgetting me.
**BRONT:** Stay behind me. `[d20 → 17]` I take the hit meant for you. That's what the armor's for.
**MAB:** Bront's Second Wind kicks in as he drops low — he stands back up steaming and furious.
**SABLE:** Finish the wrapped one! `[d20 → 10]` ...miss. Slippery corpse.
**FIFE:** Allow me the closing verse! `[d20 → 15 hit]` The Mummy folds into ash and old bandages.
**MAB:** The Wight looks at the pile that used to be its partner, then at all four of you, and thinks better of it. It sinks back under. 22 gold in the coffin, and a map scrap: an arrow, pointing deeper.

## §4 Approach — "The Serpent's Wards" (Yuan-ti Malison)

**MAB:** The bog narrows into a causeway lined with carved snakes, their eyes set with green glass. Every tenth stone is warm. Something has been expecting you.
**QUILL:** Yuan-ti Malison. 66 HP, and those wards — step wrong and it's 2d6 poison, no save.
**SABLE:** So don't step wrong. Easy. `[d20 → 20]`
**MAB:** Natural twenty. You read the warm stones like sheet music and dance the whole party through untouched.
**FIFE:** That was ART. I'm writing that down.
**MAB:** But the causeway ends at the serpent-priest itself, coiled and waiting. It strikes first. `[d20 → 18 hit]` Fangs find Bront's shoulder.
**BRONT:** Felt that. `[d20 → 13 hit, d8 → 7]` Feel this.
**QUILL:** Its scales resist cold, so no firebolt — I'll hex it instead. `[d20 → 11]` Partial. It's slower now.
**FIFE:** A slow snake is a sad snake! `[d20 → 9 miss]` ...my rhyme was better than my aim.
**SABLE:** Cover me. `[d20 → 18 hit]` Sneak attack — right between the wards. `[d6 → 5]`
**MAB:** The Yuan-ti hisses a spell; the air itself turns against Quill. `[d20 → 15 hit]` 9 damage.
**QUILL:** Rude! But instructive. `[d20 → 20]`
**MAB:** Natural twenty. Your hex detonates — the serpent-priest comes apart in a burst of green light. It drops a scale amulet worth 40 gold, and beyond it: the black mere, still as glass.
**BRONT:** That's the place. Whatever's under there, it's big.

## §5 Boss — "Many Heads" (Hydra)

**MAB:** The mere doesn't ripple. It rises. Water sheets off a body the size of a barn, and then the heads come up — one, three, five — each on a neck like a drowned tree.
**QUILL:** HYDRA. 120 HP. Cut a head and two grow unless the stump is burned — that's the whole trick, that's the ONLY trick, please remember it!
**FIFE:** Five heads, four heroes. Finally, a fair fight! `[d20 → 18 hit]`
**BRONT:** Opening swing. `[d20 → 1]`
**MAB:** Natural one. Bront's boot finds the one slick rock on the whole shore; he goes down under a head as it slams the mud where he stood.
**BRONT:** ...I'm fine. That's going to bruise.
**SABLE:** Torches, now! `[d20 → 17 hit, d6 → 3]` I cut — Fife, burn it!
**FIFE:** With PLEASURE! `[d20 → 13 hit]` One stump, cauterized. One head down, and it stays down!
**QUILL:** It's learning — it's guarding its necks. DC 15 to land a clean cut now! `[d20 → 2 miss]` ...I did not land a clean cut.
**MAB:** Two heads dive and come up under the party. `[d20 → 9 miss, d20 → 18 hit]` One misses; one catches Sable across the ribs for 11.
**SABLE:** Worth it. Still got the torch.
**BRONT:** Back up. Together this time. `[d20 → 19 hit]` `[d8 → 8]`
**FIFE:** Burn! `[d20 → 11]` ...too slow, it grew back. Two heads again. This is math I don't like.
**QUILL:** Then let me do the math. Hex the water — every head shares one heart! `[d20 → 14 hit]`
**MAB:** The hex sinks in. The whole beast shudders. One clean shot at the heart will end it. Sable — it's yours.
**SABLE:** For the ribs. For the arm. For the whole rotten swamp. `[d20 → 20]`
**MAB:** Natural twenty. The dagger goes in to the hilt and the Hydra comes down like a falling tower — a wave of black water, then silence, then, at last, the frogs. In the mud where it sank: 250 gold, a pearl the size of a fist, and a way out.
**FIFE:** THAT is the finale. The Ballad of the Many Heads! Verse one begins—
**BRONT:** Later.
**SABLE:** Much later.
