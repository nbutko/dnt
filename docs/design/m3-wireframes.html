M3 Wireframes — The Character Sheet

Stylized, low-art mocks for the D&D layer: character creation, the reworked Inn, the Shop, the encounter d20, chest premonitions, item & weapon handling, and the new in-battle "juice" moments.

Same palette and type tokens as the M0 wireframes and the visual spec — every screen is CSS shapes, no images. Hand-authored (not a Claude Design canvas), but built to drop into the same review flow. Read against the M3 scope doc; section anchors below link back to it. Numbers shown (scores, prices, DCs) are illustrative placeholders — the real values are M5 tuning knobs.

4
The encounter d20 — rolled once, before you type

The marquee moment. When you commit to a fight, the encounter reveal modal rolls one dramatic d20 (scope §encounter-roll). The total picks which of the dungeon's three prompt-difficulty bands you'll type; a natural 1 fumbles, a natural 20 inspires. It happens before the clock starts, so it can be as big as we like.

5
Premonitions — sensing mimics & reading a chokepoint

A hidden d20 + WIS + proficiency vs the dungeon's mimic deception DC (scope §mimic-sense). Only a mimic ever trips it — a real chest never does — so a warning is a trustworthy "this one's a mimic," while no warning stays ambiguous. WIS also gives a soft threat read on hidden chokepoints. The check itself stays hidden; only a success surfaces.

6
Item consumption — from the dungeon map, before a fight

Hard rule from the brief: items are never used during a round — a round is pure typing (scope §items). You open the bag from the dungeon map, spend a consumable, and its buff applies to your next fight (or the rest of the run). All active buffs clear when the run ends. Owned items persist; only the buff is per-dungeon. Design call: the bag is a drawer you expand from a map button, not an always-on panel — the branching graph needs the room, and prepping is a deliberate beat, not constant clutter. (The active-buffs strip, being tiny and glanceable, does stay pinned.)

7
Weapon assignment — the Inn's Armory (between dungeons only)

Unlike consumables, a weapon stays with you across dungeons until you swap it (scope §weapons). Design call: you equip only at the Inn, between runs — never mid-dungeon — so gear choice is a commitment you live with for the whole run, not a per-fight fiddle. It's the Armory tab of the reworked Inn (turn 2). A weapon never adds an in-battle action; it reshapes the math via its die, governing ability, and crit range. Selecting one shows a side-by-side compare.

What I'd add — the in-battle "juice" moments (the forgotten screen)
