Combat Tuning Implementation Plan

Turning §8 of the tuning doc into a story-by-story build — the keystone first, each knob re-measured by the sim before the next one moves.

The tuning-targets doc did steps B.1 (write the target surface) and A (measure the real surface with content-pipeline/retune-sweep.ts) and found the gap: §7's four findings — hits-to-kill collapsed toward 1 at high tiers (an uncapped lengthFactor one-shots bosses), the level↔wpm tradeoff inverts, a wide class spread, and a low end that's also broken (D1 unwinnable for a beginner). This doc is step B.2: the ordered build that closes that gap, one committable story at a time. It assumes the §8 remediation plan as its spec and does not re-argue it — read §7/§8 first; this doc answers how and in what order, not why.

The INT→roll fix already landed (commit 9dfb9f8) — INT is now a nudge on the encounter d20 instead of a hard intTierCap, so every dungeon serves its whole textTierRange and level-appropriate content is always reachable. That fix is what unmasked the uncapped-lengthFactor problem this plan fixes (removing the cap stopped it from accidentally penalizing fixed heroes at high tiers — see the parked sweepTierMonotonicity comment in combat-invariants.test.ts). Everything below builds on that commit.

The one working rule for this whole pass: measure every move

This is a tuning pass, not a feature build — the risk isn't "does it compile," it's "did the number make the fight feel right." So the loop is fixed for every story:

  1. Make the change (engine shape and/or a knob in config/ or content/monsters.json).
  2. Re-run npx tsx content-pipeline/retune-sweep.ts and read the measured surface against §2/§3's targets — on-track a comfortable win at ~target hits-to-kill, ahead marginal, behind safe, the impossible corners actually losing.
  3. Only commit once the sweep shows that story's slice of the surface converging. Numbers are landed by measurement, never by derivation-on-paper (same discipline as M0's baseDamage and M3's damageScale).
config/combat.ts is deliberately unfrozen for this pass — and only this pass. The standing invariant is that its committed values move only in "a deliberate, reviewed change, not a drive-by." This retune is that reviewed change: it was scoped in B.1, measured in A, and planned here. The knobs that move (a new lengthFactorCap, monster HP, the ability magnitudes in config/abilities.ts) are all authored against the sim in the stories below. When the pass closes (Story 5), the file re-freezes at its new committed values.

Where the knobs live

The retune touches four homes, and keeping each change in its home is what keeps the pass reviewable. Nothing here reaches into the combat engine/ to hard-code a number — the engine grows shapes (a cap function, a crit read), the tuned values stay in config/content.

HomeWhat moves here
engine/damage.ts Shape only. Soft-cap lengthFactor (Story 1); actually read the crit subsystem the CLAUDE.md gotcha flags as dead — rollIsCrit/computeDamage honouring critChanceBonus, a weapon's critRange, and the crit-boost item's critDamageMult (Story 3).
config/combat.ts The new lengthFactorCap knob (Story 1); any early-boss cadence tweak that isn't per-monster (Story 4).
config/abilities.ts The per-point magnitudes — wisTimeBudgetMsPerMod, chaIntimidateWpmCutPctPerMod (and a new CHA-charm magnitude), dexCritChancePctPerMod, strDamagePctPerMod, damageScale — re-landed against the corrected fight length (Stories 2–3). These are still the "M5 is a values edit" corral; this pass sets their floor.
content/monsters.json + config/weapons.ts / config/items.ts Boss & regular HP authored against the hit distribution (Story 1); the weapon ladder extended the length of the game and a Wizard wand-line (Story 3); defensive/HP gear and the crit-boost item's real payload (Story 3).

The seam holds — CHA-charm is the one genuinely new mechanic

Almost every story here is a values change or a shape the engine already has a socket for. Two exceptions are real code:

  • The crit subsystem (Story 3) is computed but ignored today — modifiers.ts already produces critChanceBonus, and weapons already carry critRange; damage.ts just never reads them (flat combat.criticalChance / ×2). Wiring it is deleting a hard-coded constant in favour of a field that already flows through resolveModifiers() — the seam is unchanged.
  • CHA-as-charm (Story 2) is the one new lever. Today CHA only cuts the monster's effective wpm (intimidateWpmCut). The tuning doc wants a second linear time-buyer: charm the monster into mistyping its own line (cutting its effective accuracy/attention), distinct from slowing it. That's a new field on PlayerModifiers and a new read in the monster-typing path — but it flows the same route (resolveModifiers() → battle store → the monster's simulated typist), so it grows the seam, doesn't route around it.

Stories & tasks

Strict order — Story 1 is the keystone and everything else is tuned on top of the fight length it restores, so it cannot move later. Stories 2–4 are independent of each other once 1 lands (they can reorder), and Story 5 closes the pass. Each is one commit with a check-in between, same as always — never batched.

Story 1 — Keystone: cap lengthFactor + author HP against the hit distribution fixes Findings 1 & 2, shrinks 3

The single highest-leverage change in the pass. One capped multiplier + re-authored boss HP turns the htk≈1 one-shot collapse back into a multi-prompt fight, which un-inverts the whole tradeoff surface. Everything after this tunes against the fight length it sets — so land it, and the sim, before anything else.

  • Soft-cap lengthFactor (engine/damage.ts): today it's 1 + (charCount − referenceLength)/referenceLength, floored at 0.25 and uncapped above — a 2000-char boss prompt yields a ~166× hit. Give it a ceiling with diminishing returns above reference (a soft knee, not a hard clip, so longer prompts still reward but can't run away). Add the ceiling as combat.lengthFactorCap — a knob in config/combat.ts, not a literal in the engine. computeMonsterDamage shares lengthFactor, so the monster is capped by the same change (correct — the collapse is symmetric).
  • Author HP against the distribution, not a point estimate (content/monsters.json). A "reference hit" is a range — weak roll (min dice, no crit) to strong roll (max dice + crit) — and the spread widens with level. Add a helper to engine/sim/balance.ts that reports, for a given (class, level, weapon, tier), the weak / average / strong hit against the capped lengthFactor, then set each boss's HP so both tails behave:
    • strong tail never one-shots — max dice + crit stays strictly below full HP;
    • weak tail doesn't slog — an average hit is a meaningful fraction of HP, so the fight lands near its target #prompts (K).
    The crisp low-K rule: two weak hits win, one strong hit doesn't; generalized, ~K average hits win and the strongest single hit stays well short of a one-shot. The cap exists precisely to compress the weak→strong spread enough that both bounds hold at once — so tune the cap and the HP together, iterating on the sweep, not in sequence.
  • Regulars too, lightly. The finding table shows even low-tier bosses land at htk ~1.5–2.6 against a 4–6 target — HP is universally low relative to per-hit damage, so the re-authoring pass covers the regular roster on the same rule (a regular is a shorter version of the same fight), not just bosses.
  • Tests: a unit test pinning the cap's knee (a very long prompt's lengthFactor is bounded by lengthFactorCap, a reference-length prompt is still ~1, a short one still floors at 0.25); the balance helper's weak/strong bounds are asserted for a sample boss.

Done when: the sweep shows on-track boss fights landing at ~target hits-to-kill (not 1.0) across D1–D11, no corner one-shotting, and the level↔wpm tradeoff reading the right way round (behind safer than ahead, not the inversion §7 measured) — verified by re-running retune-sweep.ts.

Landed: lengthFactor soft-capped by a rational (Michaelis–Menten) knee asymptoting to combat.lengthFactorCap = 20 (chosen over an exponential knee, which saturated tiers 12–14 to the same value across this game's ~150× length range); all 11 boss HP + the 47-monster regular roster re-authored via balance.ts's new hitMagnitudes helper. Sweep: htk floor ~2.0 (no more 1.0 one-shots), tradeoff un-inverted (behind ≥ ahead everywhere; D9 was behind 40 / ahead 95 → now behind 98 / ahead 88). Known residuals handed to later stories: D1 low-end still unwinnable (Story 4), Wizard spread + D9–D11 htk running under target because weaponForTierLevel plateaus at tier-3 (Story 3).

Story 2 — Make the two linear levers strong: WIS (your clock) + CHA (charm) fixes Finding 2's behind corner

Finding 2's two-axis insight: at the fight length Story 1 restores, the discrete hit-count axis is still coarse near the target K, so WIS and CHA — the only linear tempo levers — are what make the slow-typing, over-leveled behind / attrition corner winnable. They must be meaningful magnitudes, and CHA needs a second dimension.

  • WIS — lengthen your clock. wisTimeBudgetMsPerMod already exists (config/abilities.ts, +ms per WIS mod, read in modifiers.ts). Re-land its magnitude against the corrected fight length so a WIS-heavy build buys a genuinely more leisurely cadence at the current tier (the "less frenetic" leveling feel from §3), not a token few hundred ms.
  • CHA — charm, a second linear time-buyer. Today CHA only cuts the monster's effective wpm (chaIntimidateWpmCutPctPerMod → intimidateWpmCut, applied in engine/sim/balance.ts as monster.wpm × (1 − cut)). Add charm: cut the monster's effective accuracy/attention so it fumbles its own line more often — a lever distinct from slowing it (WIS lengthens your clock; charm shortens the monster's effective output). Concretely: a new magnitude in config/abilities.ts, a new PlayerModifiers field in engine/character/modifiers.ts, and a read in the monster-typing path (the monster's simulated typist / computeMonsterDamage cadence) — routed through resolveModifiers(), per the seam note.
  • Tune after Story 1. These magnitudes only make sense against a fight that lasts more than one hit, so land the numbers after the keystone, measuring the behind corner (over-leveled, slow) climbing from §7's 3–40% back to "safe" on the sweep.
  • Tests: a higher CHA lowers the monster's effective accuracy (charm) monotonically; a higher WIS raises the player time budget monotonically; both flow through resolveModifiers into a simulated fight and measurably lengthen survival.

Done when: a CHA+WIS "behind" build at the §2 behind-corner level/wpm wins safely on the sweep where §7 had it losing, and the two levers read as smooth/linear (each point of WIS or CHA moves survival by a steady amount, not in lumpy steps).

Landed: WIS wisTimeBudgetMsPerMod 300 → 1200 (+3.6s of budget at +3 WIS, enough to dent speedBonus/expire outcomes instead of rounding away). CHA gains charm: new chaCharmAccuracyCutPctPerMod (0.05 → −25% monster accuracy at +5 CHA) → a new PlayerModifiers.charmAccuracyCut, computed in modifiers.ts and applied in battle-store.ts's intimidatedMonster() (mirrored in balance.ts's sim, since engine/ can't import state/) — it cuts the monster's effective accuracy the same way intimidateWpmCut cuts its wpm, read at monster-typing.ts's per-char typo roll. Flows through resolveModifiers(). Targeted behind-corner check (throwaway, not committed): a +5 WIS/+5 CHA build vs a mod-0 build at identical level/wpm/weapon — D1 0 → 98%, D5 73 → 100%, D6 54 → 100%, D9 75 → 100% (D11 already saturated). Main sweep un-regressed (htk floor still ~2.0, tradeoff still un-inverted); class-favored CHA/WIS builds (Rogue/Bard) picked up marginal corners while Fighter/Wizard were near-unchanged — a class-appropriate spread, not a blanket buff. Exact behind-corner feel left for user verification.

Story 3 — Gear ladder + Wizard wand-line + crit wiring fixes Finding 3

Finding 3's class spread (on-track Wizard 25–90% vs Fighter/Rogue/Bard 88–100%) has two causes: the wand is a d6 with no upgrade path, and the discrete hit-count axis has nowhere to grow at high level because the weapon ladder stops at tier-3. This story gives that axis somewhere to go and turns the dead crit subsystem into a real, tunable quantity — which also makes Story 1's "strong tail" a thing you can tune instead of a flat 10%/×2.

  • Extend the weapon ladder the length of the game (config/weapons.ts): today eight weapons top out at tier-3 (weaponForTierLevel's ladder stops there — the sweep header flags it). Add higher-tier weapons per governing ability (STR, DEX, INT) so every class has an upgrade path at every tier, priced into the coin economy.
  • Give the Wizard a wand-line. A d6 wand with no successor is why Wizard loses; add higher-die INT weapons so the Arcane Mind's 3-dice crit has something to scale on.
  • Wire the dead crit subsystem (engine/damage.ts, the CLAUDE.md gotcha): rollIsCrit must read combat.criticalChance + critChanceBonus (DEX's dexCritChancePctPerMod, already computed into PlayerModifiers) and a weapon's critRange; the crit-boost item (Oil of Sharpness) must actually apply its critDamageMult. This restores the Rogue identity and makes Oil of Sharpness stop being a no-op — and gives Story 1's strong-tail bound a real knob.
  • Add persistent defense / HP gear so survivability is a purchasable axis (not only a CON/leveling one), and price items so a build's flaw can be counter-balanced at each tier — the "latitude to add items" the design discussion asked for.
  • Re-park nothing: wiring crit means the fumbleDamageMultiplier / noCrits paths in damage.ts finally have live callers — check the encounter fumble/inspired flags actually reach them (the M3 plumbing gap noted in rollIsCrit's comment).
  • Tests: DEX raises measured crit rate; a wider critRange weapon crits more often; Oil of Sharpness raises crit damage; a Wizard with the wand-line closes most of the on-track gap to the other classes on the sweep.

Done when: the sweep's per-class spread at on-track sits inside a reasonable band (Wizard no longer an outlier), DEX/critRange/Oil of Sharpness demonstrably change crit behavior, and the weapon ladder offers an upgrade at every dungeon.

Landed: 7 new weapons on three ability lines (STR greatsword+1/+2, DEX fine-rapier+1/+2, INT wand+1/+2/+3 — the Wizard wand-line) at level 5/9/12 breakpoints; weaponForTierLevel now climbs the ladder so every class passes tier-3 by ~level 12. Crit subsystem wired in damage.ts: effective chance = combat.criticalChance + critChanceBonus (DEX + items) + (20−critRange)/20, and Oil of Sharpness's critDamageMult now multiplies crit baseHit (was a no-op) — both threaded through battle.ts/battle-store.ts/balance.ts, which had silently dropped the already-computed PlayerModifiers fields. Defense gear shipped as ring-of-protection (rest-of-dungeon +10% HP / −10% damage-taken via the existing buff pipeline → new PlayerModifiers.damageReductionPct); a full persistent armor equip-slot + UI deliberately deferred (favor simple). Fumble/inspired paths were found already fully wired — only stale "not wired" comments were corrected. Boss HP: only Kraken (1257→1650) and Tarrasque (1450→1680) nudged up now that weapons scale (the D9–D11 gap Story 1 parked); D1–D9 already above the ~2.0 htk floor, left as-is. Save-safety: a ?? 0 guard added to the buyItem/consumeItem reducers so an older v3 save lacking the new item key buys from 0 rather than NaN. Sweep: Wizard on-track win% climbed sharply (e.g. D9 38→68), no one-shots reappeared (floor ~1.9–2.0), tradeoff still un-inverted. Flagged for user verification: Wizard remains the weakest class (structural — lower HP die + Arcane Mind pulling harder prompts) and Fighter trails Rogue/Bard more than Finding 3 framed — residual class balance is feel-tuning, not reachable by the ladder/crit work alone.

Story 4 — Low-end survivability: make the first boss a fair first fight fixes Finding 4

The opposite failure from the top end: D1 on-track (L2 @ 10 wpm) wins ~3% for every class — a beginner literally cannot clear the first boss the anchors say should be easy. At L2 there's too little HP to survive to the second hit against the boss's cadence. Widen the bottom of the band without touching the top.

  • More low-level HP and/or a gentler early-boss cadence: raise the level-1–3 HP grant (config/leveling.ts / modifiers.ts's totalHpForLevel) and/or soften D1–D2 boss wpm/accuracy in content/monsters.json so the first fights are survivable at the anchor speed. Keep the change local to the low end — Story 1 already set the top, and this must not re-open a one-shot there.
  • Check leveling reads as "less frenetic," not "fewer prompts" (the design correction): a gained level should mainly buy HP margin and timer headroom at the current tier, making the next tier approachable — verify the low-end HP curve delivers that feel on the sweep, not just a higher win %.
  • Tests / measurement: re-run the sweep focused on the low corners (D1–D3, on-track and behind) and confirm the on-track beginner clears D1 comfortably while the ahead corner stays marginal-not-trivial.

Done when: a ~10-wpm, level-2 typist clears the D1 boss on the sweep (a fair first fight, not a wall), with no regression to the capped top end.

Story 5 — Re-enable the parked invariants & final re-sweep closes the pass

Two combat invariants were parked (it.skip) precisely on this retune. With the surface converged, re-shape them against the §3 targets, un-skip, and confirm the whole surface holds — then re-freeze the config.

  • sweepHitsToKillBand (combat-invariants.test.ts): the flat [1.2, 26] band assumed uniformly short prompts; re-express it against the new few-big-prompts design (per-tier target #prompts from §3), then un-skip.
  • sweepTierMonotonicity (same file): parked because the uncapped lengthFactor made the tier-6 and tier-11 bosses land at fewer hits-to-kill than the milestone below them (the boss-length spike outrunning HP growth). Story 1's cap + HP authoring is exactly its fix — confirm a higher tier is never easier for a fixed hero, then un-skip.
  • Full re-sweep & sign-off: run retune-sweep.ts one last time and check the whole surface against §2/§3 — on-track a comfortable win at ~target htk, ahead marginal, behind safe (via CHA+WIS), the impossible corners actually losing, and the class spread inside a reasonable band. Record the final numbers back into the tuning doc's §7 as the "after" column.
  • Re-freeze: restore the "config/combat.ts stays at committed values" invariant now that the reviewed change is complete (update the file's comment / CLAUDE.md gotcha if the frozen set changed).

Done when: both parked invariants are un-skipped and green, the full sweep matches the target surface, and the tuning doc records the converged "after" numbers.

Adjacent, deliberately not in this pass

  • The boss-window [N+1, N+3] rework — §3/§5 want a boss to roll one band higher than its regulars (so the final prompt spikes). The sweep currently pins each boss to a single tier (textTierRange: [boss, boss]) and this plan tunes HP against that. The multi-tier boss window is a separate, additive change on top of a converged surface — sequence it after Story 5 so it retunes a known-good baseline, not a moving one. Flagged here so it isn't forgotten, not folded in.
  • The brute / sandbag disadvantage trinket — an item that lowers your encounter roll on purpose (easier prompts, lean into STR damage) is a §5 idea that rides naturally on Story 3's item work, but it's a build-enabler, not a fix for a broken corner — defer unless Story 3's economy pass has room.
  • Final feel/pacing tuning — this pass lands the numerical floor (the surface stops being broken); the playtest-driven feel pass stays M5, a values edit on top of this floor, by design (same split as M3's Story 13 → M5).
