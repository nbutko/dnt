The rumor: a buried bell called the Sunmetal Heart, and the dragon that sleeps on it.

## §1 First Branch — "The Fork in the Sand" (Giant Scorpion)

**MAB:** The desert eats sound. You've walked since dawn. The dunes are red — blood-red, brick-red — and every so often a white curve of bone pokes through, a rib the size of a door. The heat wobbles the air. Somewhere ahead, buried, is the city of Al-Zafir. Somewhere under Al-Zafir is the Sunmetal Heart, the bell they say brings water back to dead sand. That's the rumor. The rumor is why you're sweating.

**FIFE:** And Fife does not sweat! Fife glistens.

**SABLE:** You're dripping on my map.

**MAB:** The trail forks. Left, the sand is smooth and easy. Right, it's cracked and broken, littered with those bones. Pick one.

**BRONT:** Bones mean shade. Take the right.

**QUILL:** Bones also mean something ate whatever the bones used to be. But fine. Right it is. Give me a Survival check to read the trail.

**SABLE:** Rolling. [d20 → 3] ...Three. I confidently lead us in a circle.

**MAB:** You do. Half an hour later you pass your own footprints and pretend you meant to. The smooth dune to your right shivers. Just a little. Sand runs off the top of it — sideways.

**FIFE:** Sand doesn't run sideways.

**MAB:** It does when something under it is moving. Rolls for initiative, my sweet glistening idiots.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 8] Eight. Slow off the mark.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 15] Fifteen. Sword's out.

**MAB:** Good, because the dune splits like a mouth and a **Giant Scorpion** shoulders up out of it — armor-plated, ink-black, big as a rowboat, two claws working and a tail curled high with a stinger the color of a bruise. It clacks once. That clack is the last quiet thing you'll hear for a while. Bront, it's on you — swing.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 3] ...Three. I hit the dune.

**MAB:** You hit the dune. The dune is fine. The scorpion is unimpressed. It grabs your leg in one claw — Quill, its Grapple beats a fifteen — and lifts.

**QUILL:** That's a big pincer.

**BRONT:** That's my line.

**MAB:** Sable, you're behind it. Dagger.

**SABLE:** With pleasure. [d20 → 18] Eighteen — and a sneak attack, because it's busy eating Bront. [d8 → 5] Plus [d6 → 4] plus [d6 → 6] — that's a lot of little numbers, Quill, add them up.

**QUILL:** Fifteen damage. I hate that I'm useful.

**MAB:** The dagger punches between two plates. The scorpion drops Bront and rounds on Sable, tail snapping down — [d20 → 17] seventeen, that hits — Sable, the stinger goes in. [d8 → 2] two piercing, and now roll to fight the venom.

**SABLE:** Ow — okay — [d20 → 4] four. I fail. Everything tastes like pennies.

**MAB:** Your arm goes cold and stupid. That's poisoned — you've got disadvantage till it burns off. Fife, sing him upright.

**FIFE:** Fife lifts his rapier and his voice! "Fear not, oh clumsy friends!" [d20 → 7] ...seven. My inspiring words land on absolutely no one.

**QUILL:** Wand. [d20 → 12] Twelve — good enough. [d6 → 1] One point of fire. One. The scorpion barely notices.

**MAB:** But it notices Bront getting up. He plants a boot on its tail and brings the longsword down two-handed. Roll it.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 6] Six. Miss. Again.

**MAB:** The blade skips off armor and buries in sand. This thing will not die politely. But Sable's dagger and Quill's spark have it leaking dark stuff, and it's decided you're more trouble than lunch. It backs into its hole, claws first, and is gone — a dimple in the dune, then nothing. Silence. Somebody's breathing hard. It's everybody.

**SABLE:** I'd like it noted that I did all the work.

**BRONT:** Noted. Poorly.

**MAB:** Take the right fork. The bones get bigger the farther you go.

## §2 Waypoint — "The Dry Well" (Phase Spider)

**MAB:** By dusk you reach a waypoint — an old caravanserai, half-swallowed. Four crumbling walls, a courtyard, and in the middle a stone well with a rope that goes down into black. There's a symbol carved on the rim: a bell. You're on the right road.

**FIFE:** A bell! An omen! Fife feels destiny tightening its—

**SABLE:** How much is the rope worth?

**MAB:** The rope is worth "you're a terrible person." You make camp. Quill, you keep watch — there's something wrong with the shadows in here. They don't sit still.

**QUILL:** I don't love that. Perception. [d20 → 5] Five. I love the fire and stare at it like a moth.

**MAB:** So you don't see the wall behind you ripple — like a stone dropped in a pond, but in the air. A leg comes through first. Then another. A **Phase Spider** steps out of nowhere, purple-black and wet-looking, the size of a pony, its eyes catching the firelight in a row. It's here, then it's a smear, then it's on the far wall. It blinks in and out of the world like a bad dream. Initiative.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 19] Nineteen! I'm up. Where is it — no, where WILL it be?

**MAB:** That's the whole problem, isn't it. Roll to guess.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 15] Fifteen — I throw the dagger at the empty spot where it's about to reappear. [d8 → 7] Seven damage if I'm right.

**MAB:** You're right. It shimmers into being with your dagger already in it and shrieks — a horrible glassy sound. Bront?

**BRONT:** [d20 → 10] Ten. I swing where it was.

**MAB:** Where it was is a lie. It's already gone — phased clean through your blade, through the wall, and reappears clinging above Fife with its fangs out. It bites. [d20 → 5] Five, though — it misjudges the flicker and clacks its fangs on stone an inch from Fife's ear.

**FIFE:** "Not the face!" screams Fife, heroically. Rapier! [d20 → 15] Fifteen! [d8 → 5] Five, and a flourish — Fife duels the spider up the wall like a stage villain!

**MAB:** It's genuinely working. You've backed it toward the well. Quill — this is a between-worlds thing, your kind of problem. Do something clever.

**QUILL:** I ready the wand for the instant it phases in — no swinging at ghosts. [d20 → 10] Ten. Mm. Marginal.

**MAB:** It phases in. You fire. [d6 → 3] three fire — enough to singe it, not enough to stop it. It sinks its fangs into Bront's shoulder — [d20 → 4] four, thankfully, a glancing scrape — but that venom's the nasty kind. Bront, save.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 18] Eighteen. I shrug it off.

**MAB:** You shrug it off. Of course you do. And here's your opening — it's cornered against the well rim, half-in, half-out, deciding whether to flee into the wall. Sable, you've still got a hand free.

**SABLE:** I grab the well rope, loop it, and — [d20 → 9] nine. I lasso the spider. Badly. But I lasso it.

**MAB:** Badly is enough. The loop catches a leg just as it tries to phase, and phasing while roped is a very bad time. It panics, lurches, and topples backward down the well — a long, echoing scrabble, then a wet crunch far below. The rope goes slack. The courtyard is still.

**FIFE:** Fife bows to no audience.

**QUILL:** The bell symbol's pointing down the well, you realize. That's not a well. That's a stair, flooded with sand. The city's below us.

**BRONT:** Down we go.

## §3 Second Branch — "The Tomb That Coughed" (Mummy)

**MAB:** You climb down the not-well and the temperature drops like a stone. Sand becomes floor. Floor becomes street. You're inside Al-Zafir now — a buried city, roofs pressing on your heads, cold blue dark, everything half-drowned in dust. Two ways forward. A wide avenue that slopes down toward a distant orange glow — that's warmth, that's probably the Heart. Or a side door, sealed, marked with a king's name and a warning.

**SABLE:** Warnings mean treasure.

**QUILL:** Warnings mean a reason for the warning.

**SABLE:** Yeah — treasure with a guard. Same thing. I pick the seal.

**MAB:** Thieves' Tools, then. [d20 → 7] ...seven, plus your kit — it grinds, but it gives. The door swings in on a room full of gold and grave-goods and one tall, dry, upright shape wrapped head to foot in blackened linen. It was standing here in the dark. Waiting. It turns its head. Dust falls off it like the room exhaled. A **Mummy**.

**FIFE:** I'd like to un-pick the lock.

**MAB:** Too late. Its eyes light — two coals. Initiative, and I want everyone's numbers.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 8] Eight.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 16] Sixteen. Front.

**MAB:** Bront steps up. The mummy raises one wrapped hand and speaks a word older than the city — that's its Dreadful Glare, and Bront, it's aimed at you. Save or freeze.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 11] Eleven. ...I look into its eyes.

**MAB:** And your legs turn to sand. You can't move — frightened, rooted. The mummy shuffles past your locked sword and swings a fist wrapped in centuries of rot. [d20 → 15] Fifteen, hits — [d8 → 5] five bludgeoning, and worse: rot, the mummy's curse. Bront, second save.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 12] Twelve. Holds. Barely.

**MAB:** Barely holds. But you're still frozen, glaring back at a dead king. Sable, save your friend — light or fire, the wrappings are dry as tinder.

**SABLE:** I grab a torch off the wall and shove it in its face. [d20 → 18] Eighteen! [d8 → 8] Eight fire — the linen catches!

**MAB:** It goes up along one arm — the mummy shrieks that dry, coughing shriek and beats at itself, and for a second the fear breaks and Bront can move. Fife, keep the fire going, it's working!

**FIFE:** Fife hurls his own torch with a dramatic cry — "BURN, YOUR MAJESTY!" [d20 → 13] Thirteen — [d6 → 3] three more fire! The tomb is a bonfire now!

**MAB:** Quill — big spell, finish it. Everyone's counting on the wizard.

**QUILL:** No pressure. Firebolt, straight down its throat. [d20 → 1] ...

**SABLE:** Oh no.

**QUILL:** ...One. A natural one.

**MAB:** The wand bucks in your hand. The bolt goes wide, ricochets off a golden urn, and slams into the ceiling beam holding this whole tomb up. Crack. Dust becomes a waterfall. The doorway you came in through groans and drops a ton of sand across it — sealing you IN, with a burning mummy, in a room that is now on fire and also collapsing. Well done.

**QUILL:** I panicked!

**MAB:** You panicked expensively. Roll me a group check to dig out the OTHER exit — the little servant's crawlway at the back — before the fire eats the air. Bront, you're strongest.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 14] Fourteen. I heave.

**MAB:** You heave a slab aside and a low crawlway opens, cool air pouring through. The mummy is a torch now, staggering, coming apart — it takes one last swing at Bront's back as you shove everyone through, and misses, and folds into a heap of embers and old gold. You spill out coughing into a cold corridor. Behind you the tomb finishes falling in on itself with a boom.

**SABLE:** Did anyone grab the gold?

**QUILL:** The gold is under nine tons of ceiling. Because of you.

**SABLE:** Because of ME? You shot the—

**BRONT:** Walk. The glow's ahead.

## §4 Approach — "The Hot Gate" (Fire Elemental)

**MAB:** The corridor slopes down toward that orange glow and gets warmer with every step. Then hot. Then that's-not-normal hot. The walls here are glassed — melted smooth, like something poured heat down this hall a long time ago and it never fully cooled. You come out onto a threshold: a great bronze door, sealed, the bell-symbol huge upon it. And guarding it, coiling up out of a crack in the floor, is a pillar of living flame — a **Fire Elemental**, roughly person-shaped, roughly furious, crackling and popping, the heat off it pushing you back a step.

**FIFE:** Well. At least it's not another spider.

**MAB:** It's worse than a spider. It's a doorman made of fire. Initiative.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 14] Fourteen.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 17] Seventeen.

**MAB:** Sable, first — but your dagger's metal and this thing is on fire, so think.

**SABLE:** I don't touch it. I flick sand at it — big handfuls off the floor, smother it. [d20 → 12] Twelve. Some of it lands.

**MAB:** Some of it lands and hisses to glass. The elemental dims for half a second where the sand hits — good instinct — then flares back, angrier, and its whole arm becomes a whip of flame lashing at you. [d20 → 16] Sixteen, that hits — [d6 → 6] six fire, and you're on fire now too, Sable, drop and roll.

**SABLE:** I drop! I roll! I say several words a bard shouldn't put in a song!

**FIFE:** Fife is already writing them down.

**MAB:** Bront — you've got no ranged option and it's made of the one thing your sword can't cut. What do you do?

**BRONT:** Water. The well flooded the city. There's water behind that bronze door — I heard it. I ram the door.

**MAB:** Oh, I like that. Athletics.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 11] Eleven. Plus muscle. [d20 → 5] ...second shove, five, that one bounces me off.

**MAB:** The bronze door booms but holds. The elemental takes the moment to wash over Bront — [d20 → 8] eight, misses, you get an arm up and the flame parts around your shield. Quill! You're the brains — how do we drown a fire in a desert?

**QUILL:** The crack it came from — the floor's glassed because magma's near, but there's a cistern symbol on the east wall, a water tank. If I blast the tank, not the monster, the room floods. Wand at the wall. [d20 → 16] Sixteen. [d6 → 2] Two damage — but it's a cracked old cistern wall, not a monster, and two is plenty to finish the crack.

**MAB:** The wall splits. A wave of cold, black, thousand-year-old cistern water crashes across the chamber. The Fire Elemental screams — a sound like a kettle the size of a house — and where the water hits it, it goes to steam. Fife, put it away. Big finish.

**FIFE:** FIFE HAS WAITED HIS WHOLE LIFE FOR THIS. He leaps atop the flooding water, rapier high, and cues the cistern like a conductor — "TAKE A BOW!" [d20 → 16] Sixteen! [d8 → 7] Seven — he drives the blade into the steam-wreathed core!

**MAB:** The elemental collapses into a cloud of scalding fog and a puddle of cooling glass. The room is knee-deep in water now, steaming, and the bronze door — cooled fast and cracked by the shock — sits ajar. One last thing. Sable, the water's rising and there's a current tugging at the door. Grab something. Reflex.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 1] ...A one.

**MAB:** A natural one, at the worst possible moment. The current takes your legs out. You go under, the water sucks you through the gap in the bronze door — and the rest of the party watches you vanish through it with a yelp, into whatever's on the other side, alone, ahead of them. There's a beat of horrible quiet. Then, from beyond the door, echoing up out of something very large and very deep, a voice like a landslide with a smile in it says: "...a visitor."

**BRONT:** Sable's in there. With it. Go.

## §5 Boss — "The Sunmetal Heart" (Young Brass Dragon)

**MAB:** You shoulder through the bronze door into a cavern that used to be the great hall of Al-Zafir, and it is vast — 120 feet across, ceiling lost in the dark, a floor of coins. Not scattered coins. A SEA of coins — the hoard is easily 40,000 gold pieces deep, drifts of it, and half-buried treasures poking out like shipwrecks. And rising from the middle of the pile, uncoiling one bright limb at a time, is a **Young Brass Dragon** — 30 feet of gleaming metallic bronze-gold, sand hissing off its scales, eyes like two lit lamps. Its name, it will tell you at great length, is Sarrakoth. It has Sable dangling by one bootlace from a single claw.

**SABLE:** Hi. Hello. Big fan. Please don't.

**MAB:** Sarrakoth is a brass dragon, which means before it eats you it wants to CHAT. It has 172 hit points. It is bored. It has been bored for 300 years. And behind it, hung on a frame of gold, is the Sunmetal Heart — a bell as tall as Bront, glowing faintly. The rumor was true. Initiative — and Bront, you burst in swinging, roll it.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 20] Natural twenty.

**MAB:** NATURAL TWENTY. You come through the door at a dead sprint, vault a drift of 5,000 gold coins, and bring the longsword down on Sarrakoth's foreleg before it even finishes its opening monologue — double the dice, Quill, do the math.

**QUILL:** [d8 → 5] and [d8 → 5] — that's 10 — plus [d8 → 4] and [d8 → 4] on the crit — 8 more — plus 3 STR. That's 21 damage. 172 minus 21 is 151.

**MAB:** Twenty-one damage and a very surprised dragon. It drops Sable — who lands in a pile of coins with a jingle and a curse — and swings its huge head around to look at Bront with what might be the first genuine interest it's felt this century. "Oh," it purrs. "You're going to be FUN." Sable, you're waist-deep in gold, act.

**SABLE:** I fill both hands with coins and I stay very still and I look for the ONE thing in 40,000 gold worth grabbing. [d20 → 9] Nine. Everything looks worth grabbing. That's the problem.

**MAB:** It all glitters the same. Meanwhile Sarrakoth inhales — brass dragons breathe fire in a 40-foot line, and also, alternately, a cone of sleep gas, and it hasn't decided which is more amusing. It picks fire. Everyone in a line 40 feet long and 5 feet wide, DC 14 Dexterity save for half. Roll.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 17] Seventeen. Save.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 3] Three. No save.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 2] Two! Fife saves nothing but his dignity, and not even that!

**MAB:** The fire washes down the hall. [d6 → 5] [d6 → 6] [d6 → 3] [d6 → 5] — that's 19 fire damage. Quill takes half, 9. Bront and Fife take the full 19. Bront, you're at rough shape now; Fife, you're singed to a crisp and your good cloak is ON FIRE.

**FIFE:** MY CLOAK. That cloak had SLEEVES!

**MAB:** Sarrakoth actually laughs — 300 years and someone threw fire at a brass dragon. It rears up 25 feet and comes down with both claws on Bront — two attacks. [d20 → 8] eight, miss — [d20 → 1] and a natural ONE, the second claw slams into its own hoard and buries itself in 6 feet of coins.

**BRONT:** Stuck. Now.

**MAB:** Its claw is stuck. Bront, it's pinned for one beat — go.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 5] Five. I slip on the coins.

**MAB:** You slip on 40,000 gold pieces, which is exactly the kind of thing 40,000 gold pieces are for. You go down on one knee. Sable — the claw's stuck, the neck's low, the bell is RIGHT THERE. What's the play?

**SABLE:** Forget looting it. The bell. The rumor said whoever RINGS the Sunmetal Heart brings the water back — and this whole city just flooded behind us. I climb the tail, I run the spine, I go for the bell. [d20 → 14] Fourteen.

**MAB:** Sarrakoth looks at the soaked, seated, undignified fighter who woke it up and gave it rain, and it does the last thing a boss should do — it laughs, a warm landslide of a laugh, 30 feet of brass shaking. "Keep the gold," it rumbles. "Take 8,000 pieces, take 80 — I don't care. You rang the Heart. You gave a dead city back its river." It stretches one wing toward the flooded stair.
