# Salt Cliffs & Tide Pools — full campaign (showrunner output)

Cast: MAB (GM), BRONT (Fighter), QUILL (Wizard), SABLE (Rogue), FIFE (Bard).
Dice ledger: d20 consumed in order — 12, 2, 3, 16 (§1); 10, 13, 20, 4, 3 (§2); 20, 11, 5, 18, 7 (§3); 11, 7, 15, 7, 11, 3 (§4); 1, 19, 6, 3, 5, 2, 14, 3, 19, 5, 7, 10, 14, 9, 1, 5, 6, 10, 5, 3, 2, 9, 20, 19, 10, 6, 15, 20 (§5). The throughline: a fisherman swore a bronze bell rings under the water at low tide, and where it rings, drowned gold waits. The party followed the sound down the cliffs. A Sahuagin Baron guards the wreck it hangs in.

## §1 First Branch — "The Bell Under the Water" (Merrow)

**MAB:** The path down the cliff is wet rope and old nails. Gulls scream over your heads. Below you, the sea breathes in and out of a cave mouth, and every time it breathes out — you hear it. A bell. Low and bronze and far away. It rings once. The tide pools at your feet are full of tiny crabs and one enormous footprint.

**SABLE:** That footprint is webbed.

**BRONT:** Big foot. Big frog.

**FIFE:** Fear not! Fife has faced frogs before! Fife has fought them! Fife has fed them flies!

**QUILL:** It is not a frog. Webbed feet, that stride length, this coast — that is a merrow. Sea-troll. It has thirty-four hit points and it bites for two-d-six plus four.

**SABLE:** How much is a bell worth?

**MAB:** The rumor says the bell hangs in a wreck, and the wreck is full of gold. Roll me a Perception, Sable. You lead.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 12] Twelve.

**MAB:** Twelve is enough. You spot it before it spots you — a green shape wedged in a tide pool, still as a rock, waiting for something small to walk close. It has a hook made of a boat's anchor. Bront, you are closest. It lunges.

**BRONT:** I raise the shield.

**MAB:** Roll it.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 2] Two.

**MAB:** Two. The rock is slick with kelp. Your boot slides. The shield goes up a half-beat too slow and the merrow's hook rakes your arm — [d8 → 1] one point, barely a scratch, but it grins with a mouth full of hooks.

**BRONT:** That's a rude fish.

**FIFE:** Fife draws his rapier! Fife will avenge the scratch!

**MAB:** Roll, bard.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 3] A three! A tragic three! The tide takes my footing too!

**MAB:** You lunge, you miss, you sit down hard in a tide pool. A crab bites your rear. Quill, the merrow is wide open and laughing at your friends. Do something clever.

**QUILL:** I fire my wand. Fire bolt.

**MAB:** Roll.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 16] Sixteen, and it hits.

**MAB:** It hits clean. [d6 → 6] Six points of fire, right in the wet green chest. Steam hisses off it. The merrow shrieks — an awful, gargling sound — and drops off the rock back into the sea. It is hurt. It is not dead. It leaves a trail of dark water going deeper into the cave. The bell rings again, louder now. Something down there heard the scream.

**SABLE:** I do not love that it can call for help.

**BRONT:** Then we go fast.

## §2 Waypoint — "The Kelp Witch's Deal" (Sea Hag)

**MAB:** The cave opens into a wide flat shelf of rock, half-drowned. Kelp hangs from the ceiling like green hair. In the middle sits a hut made of ship's wood and whale bone, and in the door of the hut sits an old woman. She is very green. She has too many teeth. She is knitting with fish spines.

**FIFE:** A grandmother! By the sea! How lovely! Fife bows!

**QUILL:** That is not a grandmother. That is a sea hag. Do not eat anything. Do not touch anything. Do not agree to anything. She has a stare that can frighten a strong man to the floor.

**SABLE:** Hello, granny. How much for directions?

**MAB:** She smiles wider. "The bell," she says. "You want the bell. Everyone wants the bell. I will tell you the safe way down — for a name. Give me one true name and the road opens." Roll me an Insight, Sable. What is the catch?

**SABLE:** [d20 → 10] Ten.

**MAB:** Ten. You are pretty sure the catch is that she eats the name and then eats you. But you are not certain. Fife, she is looking at you now. Her eyes are very old and very cold.

**FIFE:** Fife meets her gaze! Fife is a hero! Fife does not blink!

**MAB:** Roll a save.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 13] Thirteen! Fife holds firm!

**MAB:** Thirteen holds. Barely. Your knees shake but you stay standing, and you even manage a wink. The hag looks annoyed. She hates a brave one. Bront — while she is annoyed, you have a clean shot at her hut. What do you do?

**BRONT:** I flip the table.

**MAB:** Roll it.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 20] Twenty.

**MAB:** NATURAL TWENTY. Bront, you do not flip the table. You flip the entire hut. Whale bone and ship wood go up in the air, the hag goes with it, and she lands flat on her back in a tide pool, spines and knitting scattered across the rock. For one perfect second she is just a wet old woman in a puddle, furious, tangled in her own yarn.

**SABLE:** That is the best thing I have ever seen.

**FIFE:** Sing of it! Someone must sing of it! Fife will sing of it!

**MAB:** She scrambles up spitting seawater. She is too angry now to bargain and too embarrassed to fight five of you. "Down the left crack," she snarls, "and I hope it drowns you." Quill, you catch her eyes flick to the right crack when she says it.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 4] Four. I catch nothing. I am busy writing down the hit die of a hag.

**MAB:** Of course you are. Sable, you catch it though.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 3] Three. No I do not. I am busy stealing her knitting.

**MAB:** You steal a half-finished sock made of fish spines. It is worth nothing and it smells like low tide. The hag is gone into the kelp. Two cracks in the rock lead down. You do not know which is the lie.

**BRONT:** We pick one. We go.

## §3 Second Branch — "The Monitor at the Gate" (Kuo-toa Monitor)

**MAB:** You take the left crack. The floor slopes down. The bell is louder here, so loud you feel it in your teeth. The tunnel opens into a round room with a pool in the center, and standing in the pool — very still, arms crossed, breathing slow — is a fish-man. Not slouched and stupid like most of its kind. This one stands like a guard who has stood a long time. Its eyes are pale. Its hands are empty and that is worse.

**QUILL:** Kuo-toa monitor. It fights with its bare hands and it hits hard — bite and claws, and a stunning strike that can knock you flat. It has sixty hit points. This is the toughest thing we have met.

**FIFE:** Then Fife shall speak to it! Diplomacy! Perhaps it plays the flute!

**MAB:** Roll a Persuasion.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 20] TWENTY! Fife is magnificent!

**MAB:** Natural twenty — and Fife, it does not understand a word you say, but it understands the tune. You start to sing. The monitor tilts its head. Its cold pale eyes go soft. For one long moment the whole room hangs in the sound of your voice, and the guard of the gate sways, just a little, like a snake to a pipe. You have bought the party ten free seconds. Use them.

**SABLE:** I slip behind it while it is charmed. Sneak attack.

**MAB:** Roll.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 11] Eleven.

**MAB:** Eleven lands, just. Your dagger goes in behind the gill. [d4 → 4] four from the blade, [d6 → 5] five sneak — nine total — and the song shatters. The monitor screams and the room wakes up. Bront, it comes for you.

**BRONT:** Good. I swing.

**MAB:** Roll.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 5] Five.

**MAB:** Five is not enough. It slaps your longsword aside with the flat of its hand like you are a child. Then it hits you back — [d8 → 6] six damage, a fist like a wet brick — and Bront, make a save or you are stunned.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 18] Eighteen. I stay up.

**MAB:** You stay up. Your ears ring but your feet hold. Quill, it has its back to you and its whole attention on Bront.

**QUILL:** I aim the wand for the base of the skull. Fire bolt.

**MAB:** Roll.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 7] Seven. That misses, doesn't it.

**MAB:** That misses. The bolt scorches the wall and lights up the room, and in the flash you see the pool behind the monitor drops away into deep dark water — and something is coming up it. Something with a lot of legs.

**FIFE:** Fife regrets the singing.

**BRONT:** Hit it again. Together.

**MAB:** The four of you pile on the monitor at once, and worn down from the dagger and the fist-fight, it finally goes under the water and does not come back up. But the deep pool is churning now. Whatever the bell called, it is here.

## §4 Approach — "The Thing in the Wreck" (Chuul)

**MAB:** You climb down beside the churning pool and drop into the wreck itself. A whole ship, on its side, swallowed by the cave. Barnacles for a floor. Ribs of black wood for a ceiling. And there, at the far end, hanging from the broken mast — the bell. Bronze. Green with age. Ringing, ringing, though no hand touches it, ringing because the water moves it. And between you and the bell, unfolding out of the shadow, is a horror. Part lobster. Part nightmare. Two great claws. A nest of squirming feelers around its mouth.

**QUILL:** Chuul. That is a chuul. Ninety-three hit points, two claws for three-d-six plus four, and if it grabs you the feelers can leave you paralyzed. Do not get grabbed. I cannot say this enough. Do not get grabbed.

**SABLE:** So of course it grabs someone.

**MAB:** It reaches for the loudest one. Fife, that is you. Make a save.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 11] Eleven! Is eleven brave enough?

**MAB:** Eleven is not brave enough. The claw closes around Fife's whole body and lifts him off the barnacles, and the feelers brush his face. Fife, you feel your arms go numb, then your legs. You are held. You cannot move. You can, unfortunately, still narrate.

**FIFE:** Fife is aloft! Fife is captured! Fife is having a WONDERFUL time and would like to be put down!

**BRONT:** I cut the arm off.

**MAB:** Roll.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 7] Seven. That is a miss.

**MAB:** Seven skids off the shell. Sparks, no cut. The chuul does not even look at you. Sable, it is holding your friend up like a snack. What do you do.

**SABLE:** I go up it. I climb the claw and stab the joint.

**MAB:** Roll.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 15] Fifteen.

**MAB:** Fifteen finds the soft seam in the armor. [d4 → 3] three, plus [d6 → 4] four sneak — seven — right in the elbow joint, and the great claw spasms open. Fife drops onto the barnacles in a heap, numb as a wet rope but free.

**FIFE:** Fife has landed! Ow! Fife will feel that tomorrow!

**QUILL:** I put a fire bolt down its open mouth while the feelers are busy.

**MAB:** Roll.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 7] Seven again. Seven is my number tonight and I hate it.

**MAB:** Seven misses again, Quill. The bolt goes wide and hits the bell instead — and the bell rings, one enormous BONG that shakes the whole wreck, and every barnacle in the room rattles. Bront, in the ringing, you get one clean swing.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 11] Eleven.

**MAB:** Eleven is enough. Your longsword bites deep between two plates of shell. [d8 → 2] two damage — not much — but the chuul finally decides you are worth killing, and it turns its whole terrible front toward the party. It is bleeding. It is between you and the gold. And behind the bell, the deep water at the stern of the wreck begins to rise, and part like a curtain, and something with a spear and a crown of coral is walking up out of the dark.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 3] I try to spot what it is. Three. I see nothing but I hear it. It is singing back to the bell.

**MAB:** The chuul hears it too. And the horror — the ninety-pound nightmare of claws — flattens itself to the floor. It is afraid. Of the thing walking up behind it. That should tell you everything.

## §5 Boss — "The Baron of the Drowned Vault" (Sahuagin Baron)

**MAB:** He's tall — taller than Bront — and he's the deep, dark green of water where the light doesn't reach. Four arms. A trident in two of them, a net in the third, and the fourth is just for the crushing. A crown of living coral. And when he speaks, the bell answers him like a dog.

**MAB:** "You came for my gold," he says. "Everyone comes for my gold. Nobody leaves with it — nobody's even left with their name." He points the trident. "The bell's mine. The wreck's mine. And now — so are you."

**FIFE:** He's dramatic. Fife respects it — Fife also does the pointing thing.

**QUILL:** Sahuagin baron. Four attacks a round — that's two trident jabs, a net, and a claw — and here's the part I hate: when he's bloodied, below sixty-eight hit points, he goes into a frenzy and hits harder. Don't let this go long.

**SABLE:** So we hit him fast — got it — who's first?

**BRONT:** Me. Always me.

**MAB:** Then lead, Bront. Roll.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 1] ...One.

**MAB:** Natural one. Oh, Bront. You charge across the wet deck — and the chuul, still cowering on the floor, is right in your path. You trip over the horror. You go down face-first into the barnacles, your sword skitters away under the bell, and now you're unarmed, on the ground, at the feet of a four-armed baron who's just watched you fall over a lobster. He raises the trident. This is bad — dig us out of this.

**SABLE:** I'm on it — I grab Bront's collar and haul him back before that trident drops.

**MAB:** Roll it, Sable — this is the save.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 19] Nineteen!

**MAB:** Nineteen — you yank Bront back by the collar and the trident slams into the deck where his head just was, close enough to part his hair. He's up. He's swordless, but he's up and he's breathing. "Nice catch," the baron says, almost kind. Then he throws the net.

**MAB:** Quill, the net's coming for you — save.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 6] Six — that's a fail, isn't it.

**MAB:** That's a fail. The net wraps you up and drops you on the deck, wand-arm pinned. You're restrained — you can wriggle out but it'll cost you. Fife, your wizard's down, your fighter's disarmed. It's the bard's turn to be a hero.

**FIFE:** It always is! Fife draws breath — Fife will out-sing the bell itself!

**MAB:** Roll me the performance.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 3] A three — no! Not now — not NOW!

**MAB:** The bell drowns you out. You open your mouth and that enormous bronze BONG rolls over the wreck and swallows your voice whole, and the baron laughs — a wet, booming laugh. "Your bell now?" he says. "It's been mine since your grandfather's grandfather drowned. Try again, little singer." Sable, he's laughing — he's not looking at you.

**SABLE:** That's the only invitation I need — I go for the back of his knee.

**MAB:** Roll.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 5] Five — the deck's moving under me.

**MAB:** Five — the wreck rolls on a swell and your dagger skates off his scaly leg. He kicks you flat with the fourth arm. [d6 → 3] three damage, and you're sitting in a puddle next to your friends. This is going badly. Bront — no sword, bare hands — what've you got?

**BRONT:** I've got two fists. I hit him.

**MAB:** Roll.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 2] Two.

**MAB:** Two. You punch a wall of scales and hurt your hand more than him. He's untouched — four for four, the party's flat on the deck, and the baron plants a webbed foot on the ship's rail and looks down at all of you like a low tide leaves crabs. "Last words?" he says. Quill, still tangled in the net — it's you. Say something.

**QUILL:** I don't waste it on words. I aim the wand through the net — I don't need my arm free, I need one clear line. Fire bolt.

**MAB:** Roll it, Quill.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 14] Fourteen.

**MAB:** Fourteen — through a gap in the net, the bolt catches him square in the chest. [d6 → 2] two damage. Not much — but it's the first hit anyone's landed, and it burns, and it makes him flinch. He looks at the tangled wizard with the scorched net and — for the first time — he stops smiling. "So one of you bites," he says. Sable, the flinch bought a heartbeat.

**SABLE:** Then I'm up in the heartbeat — I roll to my sword — Bront's sword, under the bell — and I slide it to him.

**MAB:** Roll.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 3] Three — I fumble it —

**MAB:** Three — the sword spins across the wet deck, wide of Bront — but it clangs into the bell, and the bell rings, and this time the ring's on YOUR side. The baron whips around toward the sound on instinct — his own bell, calling — and he gives Bront his back. Bront. He's turned. Go.

**BRONT:** I dive for my sword.

**MAB:** Roll.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 19] Nineteen!

**MAB:** Nineteen — you slide across the barnacles on your belly, your hand closes on the hilt, and you come up swinging in one motion with the baron's spine right in front of you. Make the hit — roll again.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 5] Five.

**MAB:** Five — the swing's rushed, it rings off his crown of coral, chips of it flying — but it turns him back to face you, four arms, trident up, and now everyone's got a shot at his front instead of a knife at your back. Fife — the bell's still ringing but it's ringing for you now. Try the song again.

**FIFE:** Fife rises! Fife's voice cracks the salt air — this is Fife's moment, and Fife has been WAITING for it!

**MAB:** Roll me the performance.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 2] ...Two.

**MAB:** Two, Fife. Your voice cracks — genuinely cracks, a squeak in the middle of the high note — and it's so bad, so gloriously off-key, that the baron actually winces. That's it. That's the opening. Quill, you've worked a hand free of the net.

**QUILL:** One hand's all I need — I level the wand and I don't miss twice. Fire bolt.

**MAB:** Roll.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 9] Nine — is nine enough?

**MAB:** Nine — just barely, it grazes him, [d6 → 6] six damage, a long scorch up his flank, and now he's hurt, really hurt, and here's the thing Quill warned you about — he crosses below bloodied, and the frenzy takes him. His eyes go black. He froths. He forgets defense entirely and comes at all of you at once — but a frenzied thing doesn't dodge. Sable, he's wide open and wild.

**SABLE:** Wide open's my whole job — I go under the trident and up into the ribs.

**MAB:** This is the shot, Sable. Roll.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 20] TWENTY! Natural twenty!

**MAB:** NATURAL TWENTY. Sable — you drop under all four arms, you plant your feet on a barnacle, and you drive the dagger up under the coral crown into the soft dark place where the gills meet the throat. Crit — double the dice. [d4 → 6] and [d4 → 8] on the blade, [d6 → 5] and [d6 → 4] on the sneak — everything doubles, everything lands, and the baron freezes with a rogue hanging off his throat like a limpet. The frenzy goes out of his eyes all at once. The trident slips from two hands. The net slides from a third.

**SABLE:** How much is he worth?

**MAB:** He goes down like an anchor. The whole wreck shudders when he hits the deck, and the bell — his bell — rings one last time, and then stops. For the first time since the cliff, it's quiet. Just the water. Just your breathing. Bront, finish it — is he done?

**BRONT:** [d20 → 6] Six. I put the sword through him to be sure. He's done.

**MAB:** He's done. And behind where he stood, the stern of the wreck falls open like a chest, and there it is — the drowned gold. Coins gone green, a crown, a chest of pearls the tide's been polishing for a hundred years. The chuul, freed of its master, scuttles off into the deep water and doesn't look back. Fife, you get the last word — you've earned it.

**FIFE:** Fife would like it noted — for the song, for the ages, for the grandchildren — that Fife's voice, though it cracked, cracked at EXACTLY the right moment! It's not a flaw — it's technique! And now — Fife believes Sable said something about gold?

**QUILL:** Two thousand and forty gold, a coral crown worth six hundred, and eleven pearls at fifty each. I've already counted it. I counted it while you were singing.

**SABLE:** I love this job.

**MAB:** The tide's coming in, heroes. In an hour this whole cave's underwater. Take what you can carry — and climb.
