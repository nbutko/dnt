Cast: MAB (GM), BRONT (Fighter), QUILL (Wizard), SABLE (Rogue), FIFE (Bard).

## §1 First Branch — "The Fog Comes In With Teeth" (Vampire Nightbringer)

**MAB:** Grimmesh does not greet you so much as swallow you. You come down off the marsh road at dusk and the city rises out of the fog like a mouthful of broken teeth — crooked towers leaning shoulder to shoulder, each one taller and more tired than the last, their upper windows lost in a ceiling of wet grey cloud that never quite becomes rain. Lamplight pools yellow in the gutters and turns every puddle into a little smear of gold. Somewhere a bell is ringing the hour wrong, four clangs for a six-o'clock sky, and nobody comes to fix it. The whole place smells of coal smoke, old fish, and the sweet rot of a fruit cart abandoned days ago. You have come here chasing a rumor, and the rumor smells like money.

**MAB:** The rumor is this: that beneath Grimmesh, under the flooded cellars and the guild vaults and the bones of older cities, there is a bank that never opens its doors. They call it the Nightbank. Every coin that ever rolled down a gutter, slipped through a floorboard, or was buried by a miser who died before he could dig it up — they say it all rolls downhill to the Nightbank in the end, and something down there counts it, every night, forever. Sable has not stopped grinning since you heard it in a tavern three towns back. The trouble with a rumor that good is that you are never the only one following it.

**SABLE:** Okay, so, quick question — when we find a bank that eats every lost coin in the world, do we split it five ways, or do I get finder's fee, courier's fee, and a small emotional-distress bonus?

**BRONT:** We're not there yet.

**FIFE:** And YET! Fife the Magnificent can already hear the ballad — "The Five Who Broke the Bank!" — verse one, in which our humble rogue weeps openly at the sight of so many shiny things!

**MAB:** You've taken lodging on the First Branch, which is what the locals call the highest of the three great streets — the roads fork down into the city like branches off a rotting trunk, First on top in the fog, then the Waypoint, then the drowned streets below. Your inn is a narrow crooked thing called the Bent Nail, and its landlord will not meet your eyes. As full dark comes on, the fog thickens until the lamp across the street is only a rumor of light. And then Quill, who is reading by the window, goes very still, because the fog is moving against the wind.

**QUILL:** That's — that is not weather. Weather doesn't have intent. Everyone, the temperature just dropped a good ten degrees and there is a shape condensing out of the mist directly beneath the streetlamp. Pale. Elegant. Smiling. I would put money — appropriate, given the theme — on undead. Give me a Wisdom check to identify it.

**MAB:** Roll for Quill. `[d20 → 5]`

**QUILL:** Five. Plus my modifier, that's... still not much. I know it's dead and I know it's smiling and beyond that my brain has helpfully gone blank.

**MAB:** Then let me fill the blank in for you. The thing beneath the lamp wears the fog like a fine coat, and where its face should be there is a face — too handsome, too smooth, the color of a candle that has never been lit. It lifts one gloved hand and beckons, gentle as an usher showing you to your seat, and you feel the pull of it in your knees before you feel it in your head. This is a Vampire Nightbringer, a lesser lord of the mist, and it does not come to fight. It comes to invite. Its voice slides up through the shuttered window: "You are looking for the Nightbank. How lovely. Everyone who finds it stays forever."

**SABLE:** Hard pass on forever. Bront, do the thing where you're large at it.

**BRONT:** Gladly.

**MAB:** Bront kicks the shutters clean off their hinges and drops into the street, longsword out, and the Nightbringer's smile only widens — until Bront's blade is already moving. Roll your attack. `[d20 → 12]`

**BRONT:** Twelve, and my bonus carries it. Steel meets fog.

**MAB:** It does. The Nightbringer tries to turn to mist and half-succeeds, so your longsword passes through a shoulder that is only partly there — but partly is enough. Roll the die. `[d8 → 2]` Two points. A thin line of shadow opens across its coat and it hisses, genuinely surprised that the big quiet one bites. It lashes back, quick as a slammed door, raking gloved claws at Bront's throat. `[d20 → 2]` And that is a two. The Nightbringer, so composed a moment ago, slips on the wet cobbles of its own city and clatters into a rain barrel like a drunk uncle at a wedding.

**FIFE:** THE MIST-LORD FALLS UPON A BARREL! Fife shall remember this always! Now — a rapier for the memory book! `[d20 → 6]`

**MAB:** Six is not quite enough, Fife; your rapier sings a beautiful note off the lamppost and lights a spark but no blood. Sable, you're up top still at the window — you have the high angle and a dagger.

**SABLE:** High angle, low morals, my favorite combination. `[d20 → 2]`

**MAB:** Another two. Sable, in your enthusiasm you throw the dagger and your dinner knife, realize a beat too late they feel identical in the dark, and watch your good dagger sail past the vampire's ear to bury itself in the Bent Nail's front door, where the landlord will find it in the morning and quietly raise your bill. The Nightbringer rises from the barrel, dripping and furious, its politeness gone. Quill — end the introductions.

**QUILL:** With pleasure. Wand up, the words are three syllables and they taste like copper. `[d20 → 5]`

**MAB:** Five — but a wizard doesn't need to aim a wall of fire, only to be near the right barrel. Your bolt goes wide and strikes the rain barrel the vampire is standing against, and the barrel is not full of rain. It is full of the lamp-oil the innkeeper stores for winter. The whole thing goes up in a soft blue whump. Roll the fire. `[d8 → 5]` Five, and the Nightbringer shrieks — a sound like a knife on a plate — as the mist that is its body cannot hold together through flame. It comes apart in ribbons and blows away up the street, defeated but not destroyed, and its last words trail behind it like smoke: "The Waypoint. I'll tell them you're coming." The fog thins. The wrong bell rings again. You have arrived in Grimmesh, and Grimmesh knows your name.

## §2 Waypoint — "The Guildhall of the Very Patient Statue" (Clay Golem)

**MAB:** The road down to the Waypoint is a stair worn so smooth by centuries of feet that it has become a slide, and you descend it in the grey hour before a dawn that never fully arrives. The Waypoint is the middle city, the working city — a great shelf of stone where three streets meet and the guilds keep their halls. Here the fog is thinner but the crowd is thicker: chimney-sweeps and coin-clippers, a woman selling hot chestnuts that are somehow both burnt and raw, a printing-press guild clattering out pamphlets nobody reads. Everyone moves fast and looks at nothing, the way people do in a place where meeting a stranger's eye is how you lose your purse. Above it all rises the tallest, straightest building you have yet seen: the Mint Guild's counting-house, its doors banded in iron, its windows barred, a single lantern burning over the entrance like a held breath.

**MAB:** The Nightbringer, before it blew away, said the Waypoint would be told you were coming — and the Mint Guild has taken the warning seriously. As you cross the great square toward the counting-house, a beggar plucks Sable's sleeve and mutters that the way down to the Nightbank runs through the Mint's cellar, that it always has, that the guild guards the stairwell with something that does not eat and does not sleep and has stood in the dark counting the same coin since before the guild had a name. Sable, being Sable, hears "stairwell to infinite money" and stops listening after that. The counting-house door, when you reach it, is not locked. It swings open onto a hall of ledgers, and in the center of that hall, waist-deep in a drift of loose copper, stands a figure the color of riverbed clay.

**QUILL:** Oh, I know this one. I actually know this one — everybody hush. That is a Clay Golem. Roughly man-shaped, entirely NOT a man, made of packed grey clay with a slot for a mouth and a guild-seal pressed into its chest like a maker's mark. Statistically it is a wall that has opinions. It will not move until we do something it counts as theft, and then it will move faster than anything that heavy has any right to. Do NOT touch the coins.

**SABLE:** Define "touch."

**MAB:** Roll me a Dexterity check, Sable, because I saw your hand twitch. `[d20 → 5]`

**SABLE:** Five. My hand betrays me. It has always wanted this.

**MAB:** Your fingers close around a single copper piece at the edge of the drift — one coin, the smallest possible crime — and the Clay Golem's head turns with a sound like a millstone. The guild-seal on its chest flares red. Its mouth-slot grinds open and out comes not a voice but a number, spoken in a landslide: "One." It has been counting for nine hundred years and you have just made the total wrong, and a thing like that cannot abide a total being wrong. It raises a fist the size of an anvil. Roll for initiative — Bront, you're first.

**BRONT:** Between it and Sable. Where I live. `[d20 → 4]`

**MAB:** Four; your boot skids on loose copper and your mighty guarding-swing turns into a mighty guarding-flail, but you do get your body in the way, which was the whole point. The golem's fist comes down. `[d20 → 10]` Ten — it connects, but you catch it on your shield rather than your skull. The impact drives you to one knee and cracks the shield down the middle. Roll the damage against Bront. `[d8 → 2]` Two — your arm will ache for a week but the shield died so you didn't. Quill, its clay is soft to cold and hard to fire — pick wrong on purpose and see what happens.

**QUILL:** I will do the correct thing, thank you, which is to freeze the water still inside the clay. Frost, not flame. `[d20 → 15]`

**MAB:** Fifteen lands clean. Rime crawls up the golem's leg and the clay there goes brittle and pale. `[d6 → 4]` Four points, and more importantly a hairline crack now runs up its shin like a river on a map. Fife — sing at the crack.

**FIFE:** Fife does not merely sing, he PERFORMS! A rising trill to shiver the very stone — and a rapier-point delivered precisely to the flaw our wizard so kindly provided! `[d20 → 17]`

**MAB:** Seventeen, and you thread the rapier into the frozen crack like a key into a lock. `[d8 → 1]` A single point of damage — but the crack widens with a sound like a tooth breaking, and the whole golem staggers, off-balance for the first time in nine centuries. Sable — you started this. Finish it. There's a lever on the wall marked COIN CHUTE and a very heavy golem standing on a very cracked leg right above the open floor grate.

**SABLE:** Oh, I love when the room does the murder for me. I pull the lever. `[d20 → 17]`

**MAB:** Seventeen. The chute grate yawns open beneath the golem's frozen foot, the cracked leg finally gives, and nine hundred years of patience goes over sideways into the dark with a long grinding roar and a final, offended "...zero." Copper rains down the chute after it in a bright singing flood. Silence returns to the counting-house. And there, revealed behind where the golem stood, is a narrow iron door, ajar, breathing cold air up from below. Somebody has already been through it — the dust is disturbed, and there are wet footprints leading down that none of you made.

**SABLE:** ...Someone's ahead of us.

**BRONT:** Then we hurry.

## §3 Second Branch — "The One Who Came Back to Collect" (Haunting Revenant)

**MAB:** Below the Mint the city changes its nature. The iron door lets you out onto the Second Branch — the lowest of the three great streets, the drowned one, where the fog has given up being air and become water. You walk a raised stone causeway with black canal on either side, and the buildings here stand knee-deep in it, their ground floors long surrendered, their doorways lapping. Lanterns float past on little rafts, set adrift by mourners for reasons no one now living remembers, and their reflections double the light until you cannot tell the true flame from its twin in the water. It is beautiful and it is wrong, and the wet footprints you have been following lead straight down the middle of it, always just far enough ahead to be a shape and never a face.

**MAB:** Halfway along the causeway, the footprints stop. They don't fade or turn aside — they simply end, mid-stride, as though whoever left them was lifted off the earth. And in the silence after that discovery, the floating lanterns all turn at once, every raft on the black water pivoting to point its little flame at a single narrow house that leans over the canal with its windows dark. The front door of that house opens by itself. From inside comes a dragging sound, the sound of something heavy that will not be hurried, and a voice like grave-dirt poured slowly from a jar. "You took my coin," it says. "Nine hundred years I counted it, and you took it, and you gave it to the chute." Something is wearing the memory of a man and it is very, very tired of waiting.

**QUILL:** That's not the golem. The golem's timber, it went down the chute — this is — oh. Oh, this is worse. Wisdom told me and I don't like it. A Haunting Revenant. Something died with an errand unfinished and clawed its way back up out of the water specifically to complete it, and it cannot be reasoned with and it cannot be tired out and it will not stop until the errand is done or it is put down hard. And I think the errand, tonight, is us.

**MAB:** It comes out of the leaning house into the lantern-light and you see it whole: a drowned figure hung with canal weed and old chain, its eyes two coals of cold blue fire that have fixed on Sable and will not look anywhere else. It has chosen. A revenant always chooses one, and it has chosen the hand that took the coin. Sable, it comes for you first and it comes fast. But you are quick and you have had enough of this city. Roll — and I have a feeling about this one.

**SABLE:** Enough is right. I'm not being drowned by a filing error. `[d20 → 20]`

**MAB:** NATURAL TWENTY. Oh, Sable. As the revenant lunges across the causeway, you don't step back — you step *in*, under the grasping chain, and you drive both daggers up beneath its ribs with every ounce of a rogue who has decided today is not the day. Roll double for the crit. `[d8 → 3]` `[d8 → 5]` Eight points, doubled by the angle and the fury, and the cold blue fire in its eyes gutters like a candle in a slammed door. The revenant staggers back off the causeway edge, one clawed hand catching your collar to take you down with it —

**SABLE:** No! Bront!

**BRONT:** Got you.

**MAB:** Bront's hand closes on Sable's belt and hauls, and the revenant's grip tears free with a strip of your good coat, and it goes into the black water alone. But a revenant does not drown; it was already drowned. Roll to see how fast it climbs back out, everyone hold your breath. `[d20 → 10]` Ten — slow, this time. It hauls itself up a mooring post streaming weed and hate, and you have a moment. Quill, use it.

**QUILL:** Frost worked on clay. Water is just clay's ambitious cousin. I freeze the canal around the mooring post. `[d20 → 3]`

**MAB:** Three, Quill — the words come out crooked and instead of freezing the canal you freeze your own boots to the causeway, and now you're a wizard rooted to the spot by a spell that technically worked. Fife, cover him — the revenant's coming back up the post.

**FIFE:** Fife interposes himself with a FLOURISH — a rapier and a cutting couplet! "You came back wrong, you came back weak — the only thing you'll catch tonight's a CRITIQUE!" `[d20 → 16]`

**MAB:** Sixteen, and the rapier finds the coal of its left eye. `[d6 → 3]` Three points, and half its cold blue sight goes out. Now it's slow AND half-blind and clinging to a post, and Bront has finally set Sable down safe. Bront — it took your friend into the water. Say something short and end it.

**BRONT:** Errand's over. `[d20 → 18]`

**MAB:** Eighteen. Bront's longsword comes down two-handed on the chain that holds the revenant together, and nine hundred years of unfinished business finally finishes. The links part, the weed-hung shape comes undone joint by joint, and the last coal of blue fire drops into the black canal with a hiss and a curl of steam. The floating lanterns, released, all turn away at once and drift on downstream as if nothing happened. Sable, you're soaked and short one coat, and Quill is still frozen to the floor. Roll to pull your own boots free, Quill. `[d20 → 2]`

**QUILL:** Two. I fall over. I am now a wizard lying down.

**FIFE:** A LOW point in the ballad, but an honest one!

## §4 Approach — "The Landlord Who Wears a Face" (Rakshasa)

**MAB:** Past the drowned street the causeway funnels you into a tunnel, and the tunnel becomes a stair, and the stair goes down and down past strata of older Grimmesh — brick giving way to cut stone giving way to something smooth and dark that was never quarried by human hands. The air warms as you descend, which is wrong; cellars are meant to be cold. At the bottom the stair opens without warning into a room that does not belong under a rotten city: a warm parlor, richly furnished, a fire already lit, four — no, five — velvet chairs arranged as if you were expected, and a decanter of something amber catching the firelight. A gentleman rises from the largest chair to greet you. He is impeccably dressed, his smile is perfect, and his hands, when he spreads them in welcome, are turned backward at the wrist so the palms face outward and the fingers curl the wrong way.

**MAB:** "Guests," he purrs, and his voice is honey over gravel. "Down so far, so late, and so damp — please, sit, warm yourselves, I so rarely receive anyone with a pulse. You are seeking the Nightbank, of course. Everyone is. I am its landlord, in a manner of speaking, and I do hate to see ambitious young people go home empty-handed. Sit. Drink. Tell me what you would give to walk out of here rich." Quill's skin is crawling and he knows exactly why, and behind the gentleman's perfect brown eyes something older is watching you the way a cat watches a beetle it is not yet hungry enough to bother eating.

**QUILL:** Backward hands. BACKWARD HANDS. Nobody drink anything, nobody sit down, and for the love of all that's arithmetic nobody make a deal. That is a Rakshasa. A fiend wearing a person like a rented suit, and it eats debts and promises the way we eat chestnuts. It can read the top thought right off your mind and it lies with a straight face because it doesn't have a real one. Ordinary spells slide right off it. This is a fight we win with steel and cleverness or not at all.

**RAKSHASA (MAB):** "Rude," the gentleman sighs, not troubling to keep the honey in his voice now that the wizard has spoiled the surprise. "I offer hospitality and I get a lecture." His smile stretches a little too wide, past where a smile should stop, and the fur beneath the fine coat is suddenly obvious, and the eyes go from brown to the vertical gold of a tiger. "Very well. You may leave the way you entered — as a debt I collect later — or you may leave through me. Neither door is easy." Sable, he's between you and the only other exit, the round vault door behind his chair, and it's already glowing faintly with the cold of what's beyond it. Everyone, initiative.

**BRONT:** Steel it is. `[d20 → 8]`

**MAB:** Eight — your swing is honest but the Rakshasa flows around it like water around a post, tutting. It flicks two fingers and an illusion blooms: suddenly there are three of you, Bront, and three of the fiend, and none of you can tell which is which. Quill, cut through it — how?

**QUILL:** I don't dispel the illusion, I make it useless. I snuff every light in the room but my own wandtip, so there's only one true shadow to follow. `[d20 → 16]`

**MAB:** Sixteen — clever, and it works. The parlor plunges dark but for your wand, and in that single true light the Rakshasa casts exactly one shadow, and its backward hands cannot hide which way it's turned. Fife, you can see it now — go.

**FIFE:** Fife strikes from the dark with a whispered, wicked verse! `[d20 → 18]`

**MAB:** Eighteen, into the fiend's shoulder. `[d8 → 4]` Four, and it snarls, genuinely stung, gold eyes swinging to Fife with murder in them. It rakes back — `[d20 → 10]` — ten, connects, claws across Fife's arm. `[d6 → 4]` Four points; your sleeve and your skin both open and Fife's next verse comes through gritted teeth. Sable, it's fixed on the bard — get behind it.

**SABLE:** Behind a guy with backward hands is the safest place in the room. `[d20 → 6]`

**MAB:** Six — you circle wide but knock the amber decanter off its table, and it shatters loud, and the Rakshasa's head whips toward the sound — toward YOU — for just a heartbeat. Use the heartbeat, Bront. This is the opening.

**BRONT:** Take it. `[d20 → 1]`

**MAB:** ...A natural one. Oh, Bront. You commit everything to the killing blow, both hands, full weight — and the Rakshasa simply *isn't there*, one more illusion peeled off at the last instant, and your longsword buries itself to the hilt in the stone lip of the vault door and *sticks fast*. You are now unarmed, off-balance, and the real Rakshasa is behind all of you with its true claws out and a purr of triumph, and this is the worst possible moment in the worst possible room. Fife's bleeding, Sable's exposed, Bront's sword is a decoration, and the fiend rises to full height between you and the vault. Quill — it's just you and one spell and you can't hit it directly. Think sideways or we lose someone.

**QUILL:** Sideways. Sideways. It's a fiend and this is holy-adjacent water dripping from the drowned street off all of us — no. Simpler. Bront's sword is stuck in the vault door, which means it's a lever. I don't attack the Rakshasa. I superheat the sword. The blade stuck in that frozen vault door — I make it glow white-hot.

**MAB:** Roll it, and tell me you mean it. `[d8 → 4]` Four heat — and it's enough. The white-hot longsword flashes against the ice-cold vault door and the whole slab cracks along its frozen seam with a report like a cannon. The vault door swings inward under its own broken weight, and beyond it is a rush of freezing air and a darkness that even the Rakshasa turns to look at — and in that turn, that one flicker of the fiend's attention toward the thing it truly fears, Sable finally gets behind it clean.

**SABLE:** For Fife's arm and my coat. Both daggers.

**MAB:** Roll, and this time the room isn't laughing. That's the top of the Approach — hold the die for the Boss.

## §5 Boss — "The Ledger of Endless Night" (Vampire Umbral Lord)

**MAB:** Sable's daggers go into the Rakshasa's back to the hilt just as the cold from the open vault washes over the whole parlor, and the fiend — the proud, honeyed, backward-handed fiend — *flinches*, not from the daggers but from what is coming through the door, and that flinch is the end of it. Roll it home, Sable. `[d20 → 19]`

**SABLE:** Nineteen. For the coat. `[d8 → 5]` Five.

**MAB:** The Rakshasa folds around the blades with a sound that is almost relief, its rented gentleman-suit sloughing away into smoke and fur and nothing, and its last words are not a threat but a warning: "You broke the door. Now it will count *you*." And then it is gone, and the way ahead is open, and every single one of you would rather it weren't. Beyond the shattered vault lies the Nightbank at last — not a room but a cavern, vast beyond your lantern's reach, and it is full of coins. Not stacked. Not chested. Drifted, like snow, in dunes and slopes and mountains of coin that climb up into a dark that swallows the top of them, every copper and silver and gold that ever rolled down a gutter in a thousand years, and the whole shining field of it whispers as it settles, a dry metal hush, counting itself in the dark.

**MAB:** And at the center of the coin-field, throned on the highest dune, sits the thing that counts. It was a vampire once, the way a forest fire was once a spark. Now it is the Umbral Lord of Grimmesh, and the shadows of the entire buried city gather to it like iron to a lodestone — it wears darkness the way the Rakshasa wore that coat, a cloak of living black shot through with two red points where eyes should be, and it does not rise as you enter because it does not need to. Its voice fills the cavern from every direction at once, patient and vast and cold as the space between stars: "Ah. The tally that took a coin, the bard that bled, the swordsman that missed. I have your account open before me. Everyone who finds the Nightbank stays to be counted. It only hurts until you stop struggling — and then it hurts differently." The dunes of coin begin, very slowly, to slide toward it, and you realize the whole cavern is a throat.

**QUILL:** Vampire Umbral Lord. The worst thing under the worst city — it drains warmth, it commands shadow, it heals in the dark and it does NOT tire. Listen to me because I only get to say this once: it is strongest in its own shadow and weakest in true light. There is no lantern down here big enough. But we came down through a city of floating flame and frozen canals and lamp-oil barrels, and if we can make ONE great light in this pit, for ONE moment, we can put out its lamp for good. Everyone buy me that moment. Initiative — and pray.

**MAB:** Fife, you're bleeding and furious and first. The Umbral Lord reaches out a hand of pure shadow toward Bront to drink him dry. Interrupt it.

**FIFE:** FIFE THE MAGNIFICENT does not let his friends be *counted*! A song to shake the coins from the very walls! `[d20 → 19]`

**MAB:** Nineteen! Your voice rings off the coin-dunes and they answer, a rising avalanche of silver hush, and the shadow-hand recoils from the noise. `[d8 → 2]` Two damage as your rapier follows the song in — small, but you have its full attention now, and that's the job. It turns its red eyes on you and the temperature drops so hard your breath comes out as ice. Bront, it took its hand off you — get to Quill and guard him, he's our whole plan.

**BRONT:** On it. My sword's still hot. `[d20 → 15]`

**MAB:** Fifteen — you plant yourself in front of Quill with the still-glowing longsword raised, and when a lash of shadow whips out of the dark at the wizard it meets your hot blade instead and *hisses* away. `[d8 → 3]` Three points burned off the Umbral Lord's reaching dark, and it draws back, wary now of the fire you carry. Sable — the coin-dunes are moving, and there are lamp-oil casks stored down here too, the Mint floated them down the chute for a century. I can see three, half-buried, up-slope of the throne. You climb like a cat. Go get us our light.

**SABLE:** A whole century of stolen lamp-oil sitting above the scary man's head. It's like the city WANTS me to do a crime. Climbing! `[d20 → 18]`

**MAB:** Eighteen — you go up the sliding coin-slope on all fours, faster than it can pour out from under you, and reach the three oil-casks perched in the dark above the throne. You've got a knife and a century of dry rope holding them. The Umbral Lord has just now noticed you up there. Quill — this is the moment, this is the ONLY moment. Sable's got three casks of oil balanced over the boss and Bront's got a white-hot sword. What do you do?

**QUILL:** I don't cast at the vampire. I never could. I cast at the OIL. Sable, cut the rope — I'll light the fall! One spark, at the right instant, right over its throne! `[d20 → 2]`

**MAB:** ...Two, Quill. The spark spell fizzles cold in your hand — the Umbral Lord's aura of drinking-dark has snuffed your fire before it caught, and now the casks are already tipping and there's no flame to light them, and for one plunging heartbeat the whole plan is falling apart into a harmless splash of lamp-oil that will do nothing but make the boss shiny. The oil is in the air. There is no spark. Everything hangs.

**MAB:** And then, Bront — you don't say anything. You just look at the white-hot longsword in your hands, the blade you superheated in the vault door, still glowing, and you look at the falling oil, and you do the only thing four words can do. Roll it. Roll everything. `[d20 → 20]`

**BRONT:** *Natural twenty.* ...This one's for the coat.

**MAB:** NATURAL TWENTY, and the whole campaign has been waiting for it. Bront hurls the white-hot longsword end over end up into the falling curtain of lamp-oil — a spinning bar of fire-metal meeting a century of oil in the air directly above the throne of the Umbral Lord — and the Nightbank turns to *day*. Roll the fire and double it, Bront, roll it like you mean to end the world. `[d8 → 8]` `[d8 → 8]` Sixteen points of blooming, roaring, cavern-filling light, and the Umbral Lord — the thing that heals in the dark, that drinks warmth, that has counted the coins of a thousand years — is caught in the center of true fire with nowhere in all its kingdom of shadow to hide, because you took its shadow away.

**MAB:** It does not go quietly. It rears up off the throne, a mountain of screaming black unraveling in the light, and it reaches for all five of you at once with the last of its strength — but the coins are already sliding, the dunes are pouring away from the fire, the whole counted hoard collapsing inward, and the Umbral Lord of Grimmesh is dragged down into its own throat by the very treasure it hoarded, burning all the way, its two red eyes the last thing to go out.
