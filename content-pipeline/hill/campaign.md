# Wind-scoured green hills — full campaign (showrunner output)

Cast: MAB (GM), BRONT (Fighter), QUILL (Wizard), SABLE (Rogue), FIFE (Bard).
Dice ledger: d20 consumed in order — 1, 13, 5, 9, 20, 20, 6, 11, 10, 1, 10, 8, 4, 14, 12, 12, 1, 17, 10, 15, 20, 18, 13, 11, 9, 18, 15, 20, 18, 1, 17, 13, 2, 9, 18, 19, 11, 14, 12, 20 … (d8/d6/d4 pulled inline for damage).

Throughline: A shepherd swears the tallest cairn on the Hill *sings* at dusk, and that a silver horn is buried at its heart — the Whistling Cairn. The party climbs to dig it up. Every hungry thing on the Hill got there first, and the horn's keeper is bigger than all of them.

---

## §1 First Branch — "The Wrong Gorse" (Dire Wolf)

**MAB:** The Hill rolls out ahead of you, green and bald and endless, combed flat by a wind that never quite stops. Gorse grabs at your knees. Somewhere below, sheep bones bleach in the heather. The shepherd's track splits at a leaning stone — one way climbs, one way skirts a hollow full of yellow flowers.

**SABLE:** The pretty flowers or the boring climb. This is a trap and I love it. Which one's worth more?

**FIFE:** Fife the Bold chooses beauty! Onward, to the golden hollow!

**BRONT:** Bad idea.

**MAB:** Give me a Wisdom check to read the ground, Bront.

**BRONT:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 1]

**MAB:** You crouch, you squint, you say "looks fine" — and step your whole armored weight onto a mat of gorse that is not ground at all but a lid over a wolf's den. The floor drops. You go in to the ribs. And the thing that lives there is already awake, already snarling, and very much home. A **Dire Wolf** unfolds out of the dark under the hill, shoulders like a doorway, breath like a butcher's back step.

**QUILL:** For the record, that's an ambush *we* built. Dire Wolf, 37 hit points, bite for 2d6+3. It has advantage because half the party is admiring flowers.

**SABLE:** I stop admiring flowers.

**MAB:** It lunges at Bront while he's stuck. Roll to hang on.

**BRONT:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 13] Thirteen.

**MAB:** Thirteen holds. You get an arm up as the jaws close, and the bite meant for your throat crunches into your shoulder plate instead — [d8 → 1] a single point, a scrape, a scream of steel. It's furious now. It wanted meat and got a mouthful of pauldron.

**QUILL:** My turn. Firebolt, into the gorse at its feet — dry gorse, dry summer. *(rolls to hit)* [d20 → 5] Five. I miss the wolf entirely and light a bush.

**FIFE:** You lit a *bush*.

**QUILL:** The bush is now a tactical asset. It can see. It doesn't like fire.

**MAB:** True enough — the flames throw the wolf's shadow huge against the den wall and it flinches back a step, ears flat. Sable, it just gave you its flank.

**SABLE:** Finally. I drop into the hole behind it, quiet as a rumor, dagger low. *(rolls)* [d20 → 9] Nine — not sneaky, but it's busy hating a shrub. [d4 → 3] Three plus my dex, right between the ribs. It yelps. That's a good sound. That's a *losing* sound.

**MAB:** Bront, you're still waist-deep in wolf den with a wolf. End this.

**BRONT:** Longsword. Down. *(rolls)* [d20 → 20]

**FIFE:** THE HILL DELIVERS!

**MAB:** A natural twenty from a man stuck in a hole. You bring the blade over in a short ugly chop with everything your trapped legs can give it — [d8 → 3] doubled on the crit, plus your strength — and the Dire Wolf simply stops. Folds. The den goes quiet except for Quill's proud, crackling bush. You climb out smelling of dog and smoke. Off up the slope, the *other* fork — the climbing one — waits, exactly as boring and safe as Bront promised.

**BRONT:** Told you.

## §2 Waypoint — "The Singing Stones" (Harpy)

**MAB:** The climb tops out on a shelf of the Hill where a ring of standing stones leans every which way, like teeth in an old jaw. And here it is: the sound. A thin sweet note threading through the gaps, rising and falling. The shepherd was right. The cairn sings.

**FIFE:** Music! My people! Fife answers the Hill in kind — *(clears throat)* a harmony!

**QUILL:** Don't harmonize with the mystery hill-song, that's rule one of everything.

**MAB:** Too late. Fife opens his mouth, and something up on the tallest stone opens *its* mouth wider. The note wasn't the cairn. It was bait. A **Harpy** peels off the stone on ragged wings, filthy feathers, a woman's face wearing a smile that has never once meant anything kind. The song coils down into your ears like warm honey. Everyone but Fife, give me a Wisdom save — Fife, you're already humming along, you're gone.

**FIFE:** Fife walks toward the nice lady. Fife has never felt so understood.

**SABLE:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 20] Twenty! The song hits my ears and just — bounces. I've heard better buskers in worse alleys. Lady, your pitch is *flat*.

**BRONT:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 6] Six. It's a good song.

**MAB:** Bront and Fife both drift stone-eyed toward the edge of the shelf, where the ground gives way to a long, heathery, bone-strewn drop. The Harpy wants you *walking off the Hill.* Quill?

**QUILL:** Save. *(rolls)* [d20 → 11] Eleven, just clears it — barely. I clamp my hands over my ears and it's like surfacing from underwater. Harpy: 38 hit points, and it's a coward, it fights from the air. Sable, we've got maybe two turns before our fighter cheerfully strolls off a cliff.

**SABLE:** On it. I grab Bront's belt with one hand — *(rolls Strength)* [d20 → 10] ten, and he's heavy, oh he's *heavy* — I get half a fistful and dig my heels into a bone. He drags me a foot toward the edge before he stops. "Come look at the lady, Sable." No, Bront.

**MAB:** Fife's at the lip now, one boot over nothing, arms wide to embrace his new favorite singer.

**QUILL:** Firebolt at the Harpy — I need to break the song, not Fife. *(rolls)* [d20 → 1]

**FIFE:** *(dreamily)* A natural one.

**MAB:** Your bolt goes wide, wide, *wildly* wide, and strikes the tall stone the Harpy sang from — which cracks, tips, and topples off the shelf with a groan of falling rock. The Harpy shrieks — real fright this time, not song — and breaks off its lure to dodge its own collapsing perch.

**QUILL:** I meant to do the exact opposite of that but I'll take the result.

**MAB:** The broken song lets go of your heads like a hand unclenching. Fife blinks at the drop one inch from his toes.

**FIFE:** …Fife was NOT about to do that.

**BRONT:** You were.

**MAB:** The Harpy's aloft and angry. Bront, you're back — throw something.

**BRONT:** Rock. *(rolls)* [d20 → 10] Ten. [d6 → 1] One. Bad rock.

**MAB:** The rock sails past. But Sable's already up on the fallen stone, using it as a ramp, dagger out.

**SABLE:** As it wheels past low — *(rolls)* [d20 → 8] eight, ugh, I'm reaching — [d4 → 2] just two, a nick across one wing. Enough to make it think. It climbs, screaming, out over the heather, and it does not come back. Nobody harmonize with anything ever again.

**FIFE:** Fife has learned. Fife will only harmonize with *safe* singing.

## §3 Second Branch — "The Cairn Field" (Ogre)

**MAB:** Past the singing stones the Hill flattens into a field of old cairns — dozens of them, chest-high piles of grey stone, each one somebody's grave from a time nobody remembers. The wind drops here. It's very quiet. Too quiet, in the way that means something big is being still on purpose.

**SABLE:** Grave field. Grave *goods.* I'm just saying, someone loved these dead people enough to bury them with the good cutlery.

**QUILL:** You want to loot a graveyard on a haunted hill.

**SABLE:** I want to loot a graveyard *efficiently.* Which cairn's worth digging?

**MAB:** Roll me a check to spot the freshest-disturbed one.

**SABLE:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 4] Four. And in my greed I read it exactly backward. The one I pick as "recently opened, easy pickings" is recently opened because something big *sleeps* in it, having rolled the capstone off to make room for its shoulders. I lift the flat stone lid and I'm looking at a toe the size of my forearm.

**MAB:** An **Ogre** has hollowed out a burial cairn into a bed, and you just knocked on the headboard. It sits up — the whole cairn sloughs off it — and it is *enormous,* dressed in stitched hides and a necklace of sheep skulls, holding a club that used to be most of a tree.

**QUILL:** Ogre. 59 hit points. Greatclub, 2d8+4. It is not fast and it is not smart, and those are the only two nice things I can tell you.

**BRONT:** Good enough. I go.

**MAB:** Bront, you put yourself between the giant and the greedy. Attack.

**BRONT:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 14] Fourteen. Hits. [d8 → 6] Six and my strength, longsword across the shin. It's like chopping a fence post. A fence post that now hates me.

**MAB:** It roars and brings the club down where Bront stands. Roll defense — [d20 → 12] twelve against your armor, it connects. [d8 → 4] and [d8 → 7] — eleven points, plus its strength, that's a *lot.* You get the shield up and it still drives you to one knee, arm gone numb to the shoulder.

**BRONT:** That's a big club.

**QUILL:** Firebolt while it's leaning on Bront. *(rolls)* [d20 → 12] Twelve, hits its broad dumb back. [d6 → 2] Two — barely a candle against that hide, but it *turns,* which is the point. Turn toward the wizard, big fellow. Turn away from the fighter you're standing on.

**MAB:** It takes the bait — Ogres always take the bait — and lumbers a step toward Quill, club dragging.

**SABLE:** Which is when I remember I'm still standing in its bed with a dagger. I go up the cairn behind it like a ladder. *(rolls)* [d20 → 1]

**FIFE:** The rogue rolls a one and I feel it in my bones.

**MAB:** You get a boot on a loose grave-stone that is, after all, just a loose grave-stone. It rolls. You go down hard on your back inside the cairn, dagger skittering off into the rubble, wind knocked clean out of you, staring up at a very tall problem that is now looking straight *down* at you.

**SABLE:** I regret my hobbies.

**MAB:** Fife — your rogue's flat on her back under an Ogre's shadow. What's the Bard do?

**FIFE:** The Bard does the only thing a Bard can. Fife plants his rapier, throws both arms wide, and *insults it.* A rhyming couplet about its mother, its smell, and its poor taste in furniture — Vicious Mockery! *(rolls)* [d20 → 17] Seventeen! [d4 → 3] Three psychic points, and the important part: it flinches, it covers its ears, it forgets the little rogue for one whole precious second.

**MAB:** That second is yours, Bront. It's distracted, it's off-balance, its back is to you.

**BRONT:** Second Wind. Then everything. *(rolls)* [d20 → 17] Seventeen. Both hands. [d8 → 8] Eight — max — plus strength, up under the arm where the hides gap. The Ogre goes over like the tree its club came from. The cairn field shakes once and is quiet again.

**SABLE:** *(from the ground)* …Is it dead? Somebody tell me it's dead before I open my eyes.

**BRONT:** It's dead. Your dagger's over there.

**MAB:** And half-buried where the Ogre slept, catching the grey light — a curl of tarnished silver. The lip of a horn. Not *the* horn. But a piece of one, snapped off long ago. The Whistling Cairn is close. And something up the slope just noticed you have a piece of what it guards.

## §4 Approach — "The Hag's Bargain" (Green Hag)

**MAB:** The cairns thin out and the ground turns wet and mean — a boggy saddle between two shoulders of the Hill, reeking of stagnant water and something sweeter and worse underneath. Mist pools here even in daylight. In the middle of it, on a hummock, sits an old woman at a small fire, stirring a pot, humming the exact tune the cairn sings.

**FIFE:** A kindly grandmother! In the murder-bog! What luck!

**QUILL:** That is not a grandmother. Look at her hands.

**MAB:** Her hands are the green of pond-scum and they have too many knuckles. She looks up with a smile full of good teeth in a face full of bad ones. "Travelers," she says, sweet as rot. "You've come for the horn. Everyone comes for the horn. I can *give* it to you, dearies — one little trade." A **Green Hag**, and she'd rather cheat you than fight you, which somehow is worse.

**SABLE:** A trade. Now you're speaking my language. What's the price, granny? And what's the horn actually *worth?*

**MAB:** "Only a memory," she purrs. "Your happiest one. You won't miss it — how could you, once it's gone?" Sable, roll me an Insight check to see the hook inside the worm.

**SABLE:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 13] Thirteen. And thirteen's enough to see her eyes. She's not offering a trade, she's offering a *first* trade — the kind that leaves you owing a second, and a third, until there's nothing left of you but debt. I've run this con. I've run it *better.* "Pass," I tell her. "We'll dig it up ourselves."

**MAB:** Her smile doesn't move but everything behind it does. "Rude," she hisses, and the fire goes out, and the mist stands up like a wall. She's fighting now. Green Hag — 82 hit points, and she'll use that mist and those illusions before her claws.

**QUILL:** 82. She's the toughest thing we've hit. Everyone stay in shouting range or the mist splits us up and she eats us one at a time.

**BRONT:** Where is she.

**MAB:** That's the trouble. There are suddenly *four* of her, one in each direction, all humming. Illusions. Bront, pick one and swing — trust your gut.

**BRONT:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 11] Eleven — I guess wrong. My blade goes through a hag made of fog and I stumble into the reeds, and the real one laughs from somewhere behind my left ear.

**MAB:** She rakes you from the mist for it. [d20 → 9] — nine, glances off your back plate. [d6 → 2] just two, but her nails leave a green stain that itches like a lie.

**FIFE:** Fife will find her! A performer knows a *real* voice from an echo! I close my eyes and listen for the one that breathes between notes. *(rolls)* [d20 → 18] Eighteen! There — the third hag, the one to the right, she took a breath before that last laugh. Illusions don't breathe! "SABLE! RIGHT! THE ONE THAT BREATHES!"

**MAB:** Fife's called the true one. She snarls, dropping the trick, and now everyone can see her. Sable — you've got the angle and the enemy's rattled. Go.

**SABLE:** This is the shot. Fog's down, she's solid, she's *cheated* and I hate a cheater. *(rolls)* [d20 → 19] Nineteen! Dagger in the meat of her shoulder — [d4 → 1] only one on the die, but sneak-attack rides on top of it, so it's plenty. She *shrieks,* and it's not a spell this time, it's just pain.

**QUILL:** And now I can see her, which means I can hit her. Firebolt, no mist to hide in. *(rolls)* [d20 → 1]

**FIFE:** Quill. QUILL. Your ones have a rhythm.

**QUILL:** The bolt slips my grip at the worst instant and detonates against the wet ground at *my own feet* — [d6 → 5] five, to me, singeing my robe and my dignity in equal measure. I am a professional and this is a professional's bad day.

**MAB:** The Hag seizes on it — a hurt wizard is her favorite meal — and lunges at Quill through the smoke. But she has to cross Bront to get there.

**BRONT:** She does not cross me. *(rolls)* [d20 → 9] Nine — just enough. [d8 → 1] one on the die, but I turn her, shoulder to hip, and shove her off her line. She stumbles into the pot she was so proud of. It tips. Whatever's in it steams and stinks.

**MAB:** Bleeding, shoved, her tricks spent and her stew spilled, the Green Hag makes the only smart move a cheat has left — she throws a fistful of mud, and when the mist clears she's *gone,* nothing but ripples in the black water and a promise on the wind: "The horn's keeper will thank me for the appetizer." The way up is open. The singing is very loud now.

**SABLE:** I don't love that she said "keeper." I thought *she* was the keeper.

**QUILL:** She was the toll booth. We haven't met the road yet.

## §5 Boss — "The Whistling Cairn" (Ettin)

**MAB:** You climb the last shoulder of the Hill. The singing swells. And at the top stands the Whistling Cairn — a great mound of grey stone, taller than a house, and the wind pours through a hundred gaps in it to make that one endless song. Wedged in its heart, catching the last of the light, is the silver horn. Whole. Waiting. And curled around the whole mound, both heads snoring in ragged harmony — *that* was the song — is its keeper.

**QUILL:** Two heads. Oh, no. Two heads.

**MAB:** An **Ettin.** Two skulls on one vast body, four eyes, two clubs, one very bad temper split in two directions. The left head wakes first. Then the right head wakes and blames the left. It stands. It fills the sky. This is the biggest thing on the Hill, and it sleeps on the treasure because the treasure is *its.*

**QUILL:** 85 hit points. Two attacks a turn, one per head, 2d8 each. Worst part: you can't sneak past a thing with a head watching every direction. We fight. Short and sharp. No mistakes.

**BRONT:** Then we start.

**FIFE:** Fife names it! The Two-Song Terror!

**MAB:** The right head roars and swings first. Everyone brace.

**BRONT:** I meet it. *(rolls)* [d20 → 6] Six. I mistime the block. The club catches my shield full-on. [d8 → 1] and [d8 → 4] — five and its strength. I skid back a yard. My arm's dead.

**MAB:** The left head grins and swings at Fife while you're staggered.

**FIFE:** Fife dives! *(rolls)* [d20 → 8] Eight — not far enough! [d8 → 3] the club clips my heel and I tumble through the heather. Fife is bruised but Fife is *singing through it.*

**SABLE:** Two heads means two necks. I go for the left one — it's lower. *(rolls)* [d20 → 14] Fourteen. [d4 → 3] Three and sneak damage under the jaw. Left head howls. Right head laughs at it. They HATE each other.

**QUILL:** That's the crack. Firebolt — right between the two faces, so each one thinks the other flinched. *(rolls)* [d20 → 7] Seven. [d6 → 6] Six. It lands. Now they're *arguing.*

**MAB:** The two heads snarl at each other, and for one turn the great body pulls itself two ways at once. It's off-balance. This is your window. Bront — take it.

**BRONT:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 19] Nineteen. Longsword, deep in the thigh. [d8 → 8] Eight. It drops to one knee. The heads stop arguing. Now they're both looking at me.

**MAB:** Both clubs come up. Both come down. Roll everything you have, Bront.

**BRONT:** *(rolls)* [d20 → 10] Ten — I get one, not both. [d8 → 2] the left club I turn. The right one lands. [d8 → 3] and [d8 → 5] — eight, plus strength. I'm down to nothing. I'm still standing. Barely.

**SABLE:** He's about to fall. Somebody finish it or we carry him home.

**FIFE:** Fife will buy the shot! One last verse — the loudest, worst, most insulting song ever aimed at two ears at once! Vicious Mockery, both heads! *(rolls)* [d20 → 18] Eighteen! [d4 → 1] one point — but they both clap their hands over their ears and the whole giant *reels,* blind and deaf and furious, its guard wide open.

**MAB:** Wide open. Sable — one dagger, one keeper, the horn behind it. Make it count.

**SABLE:** I've been waiting for this since the wolf den. I run up Bront's shield like a stair, off his shoulder, into the air — *(rolls)* [d20 → 20]

**FIFE:** NATURAL TWENTY! THE HILL IS OURS!

**MAB:** A perfect strike from the top of the sky. You come down with both hands on the dagger and drive it into the hollow of the Ettin's throat where the two necks meet. Both heads gasp the same breath. The song stops. The whole mound rings with the sudden silence. The Ettin sways — falls — and hits the Hill so hard the cairn shivers and the silver horn drops loose from the stones and rolls, gleaming, to a stop against Bront's boot.

**BRONT:** *(picks it up)* Got it.

**QUILL:** Eighty-five hit points and a natural twenty. Textbook. I'm writing this one down.

**SABLE:** How much is it *worth?*

**FIFE:** *(raising the horn to his lips)* Only one way to find the value of a magic horn! Fife shall play the first note in a thousand years!

**QUILL:** Fife, do NOT harmonize with the—

**MAB:** *(grinning)* …Roll me a check, Fife. The Hill is listening. And that, dearies, is where we'll leave it — wind on the green, four heroes on the summit, and one very loud decision hanging in the air.
