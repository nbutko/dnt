# The Whispering Wood — full campaign (showrunner output)

Cast: MAB (GM), BRONT (Fighter), QUILL (Wizard), SABLE (Rogue), FIFE (Bard).
Dice ledger: d20 consumed in order — §1: 1, 13, 5, 9, 20, 20, 6 · §2: 11, 10, 1, 10, 8, 4, 14 · §3: 12, 12, 1, 17, 10, 15, 20 · §4: 18, 13, 11, 9, 18, 15, 20 · §5: 18, 1, 17, 13, 2, 9, 18, 19, 11, 14, 12, 20, 3, 2, 1, 2, 7, 17, 15, 15, 11, 19, 9, 13, 1. (d8, d6, d4 spent inline in order.)

Throughline: In the last village before the treeline, a woodcutter swore he saw a shrine deep in the Whispering Wood — an old stone face, mouth stuffed with gold, guarded by "a lady who was mostly snake." The party follows the deer trails inward to loot the shrine. The lady is real. She is patient. And she has been waiting for dinner to walk itself in.

## §1 First Branch — "The Birds Stop Singing" (Giant Spider)

**MAB:** The forest takes you in like a mouth closing. One moment it's open meadow and warm sun; the next it's green shade, wet moss underfoot, deadfall stacked like broken furniture. Birds sing everywhere, loud and busy. You follow a deer trail down between two mossy banks. And then — all at once, like someone lifted a needle off a record — the birds stop. Every one of them. The quiet has a weight to it.

**BRONT:** Never good. Birds know things.

**SABLE:** Great. Love it here. Can we go home? What's the shrine worth again?

**FIFE:** Fear not, for FIFE does not flinch at a little silence! Fife strides boldly forth, plucking a jaunty chord to fill the — oh. Oh, that's a lot of webbing.

**MAB:** It is. The trail ahead is roofed over with grey silk, strand on strand, thick as rope, strung between two dead oaks. Wrapped bundles hang in it — a deer, a fox, something the size of a person you decide not to look at too hard. Give me a Perception check to spot what's watching you eat the silence.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 1] ...A natural one.

**MAB:** You see nothing. You are, in fact, looking the wrong way entirely — up at a pretty shaft of light — when the web behind you shivers and a Giant Spider drops onto Sable's shoulders. Eight legs, fangs like wet black thorns, and it is already biting. That's a nasty surprise round for the greedy one.

**SABLE:** I hate this forest. I hate it so much. Get it OFF —

**QUILL:** Everyone stay calm. Giant Spider, twenty-six hit points, bite is a d8 plus poison — Sable, hold still, thrashing makes the venom pump faster. Bront, it's on her back, she can't reach it.

**BRONT:** On it.

**MAB:** Bront, roll to grab and haul it off her.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 13] Thirteen.

**MAB:** Good enough. You get a fist around a bristled leg and rip the thing off Sable — it comes away with a horrible tearing sound and a mouthful of her cloak. It lands in the leaves, rights itself, and hisses at all four of you at once. Quill, its side is open. Wand.

**QUILL:** Firebolt. [d20 → 5] Five. The bolt goes wide and sets a wet log on fire, where it hisses and does nothing. Useless. The damp eats everything in this place.

**FIFE:** Then Fife shall carry the day on rhyme and rapier! To me, foul crawler! [d20 → 9] Nine — a graze! The rapier skips off its shell, but Fife's stirring couplet rallies the party's nerve, which is a kind of victory, thank you.

**MAB:** The spider lunges for the nearest warm body — Bront. Bite roll.

**BRONT:** Bounces off. Armor's good.

**MAB:** It really does bounce off — fang meets breastplate and skitters away. Now. Bront, Sable, you've both got your feet under you and the thing's out in the open. Give me your best.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 20] Twenty.

**MAB:** OH. Bront, the deadpan one, the four-word man — you plant your feet in the moss, take the longsword in both hands, and bring it down like you're splitting firewood. [d8 → 1] The die's a one but on a crit it doubles and honestly it doesn't matter — you split the Giant Spider clean in two and both halves keep twitching in the leaves. Sable, finish your friend's sentence with the dagger?

**SABLE:** [d20 → 20] Two twenties in a row! Sable drives the dagger into the twitchy front half just to be sure, then wipes it on the moss like she's insulted. [d4 → 3] Three. It stops twitching. "THAT is for the cloak."

**FIFE:** A double triumph! Fife shall write it as a ballad — "The Two-and-Twenty of the Web!"

**MAB:** [d20 → 6] The last strand of web, disturbed, drops a wrapped bundle at your feet with a soft, awful thump. Inside: an old coin purse, and inside that — [d6 → 1] a single copper piece. One. The spider's entire fortune.

**SABLE:** One copper. I nearly died for one copper.

**BRONT:** Buy yourself a birdsong.

## §2 Waypoint — "The Bear at the Stream" (Brown Bear)

**MAB:** The deer trail spills you out at a shallow stream — clear cold water sliding over round stones, banks churned to mud by a hundred hooves. Good place to rest, fill the waterskins, listen to the wood breathe. There's an old mossy waymarker stone here, half-sunk, with a carving: a face with an open mouth. The shrine's real. And it's this way. But first — Quill, you're kneeling to fill your skin. Perception.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 11] Eleven. Statistically middling.

**MAB:** Middling gets you a smell — wet dog and rotten honey — and then the huckleberry bushes across the stream stand up. They were never bushes. It's a Brown Bear, big as a cart, and it has been eating berries and it does not want to share the stream. Thirty-four hit points, folks, and a very bad mood.

**FIFE:** A bear! Fife has always wanted to charm a bear! Music, soothe this savage beast — [d20 → 10] Ten. The bear tilts its head at the lute. Considers it. Decides the lute is a threat.

**SABLE:** Nice one, Fife. Real Fife move.

**MAB:** It drops to all fours and charges the water. Sable, you're closest and you've got a dagger against something that outweighs a horse — get out of the way. Acrobatics.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 1] ...Another one. A natural one. Why me.

**MAB:** Because the forest has chosen you, Sable. You spin to dodge, your boot finds a mossy stone, and the moss wins. You go down flat in the cold stream and the bear is on you — a swat with one paw. [d6 → 1] One point of damage, which is lucky, but it's got you pinned in the shallows with your face full of river. This is bad. Party, she's under the bear.

**BRONT:** Second Wind later. Bear now.

**MAB:** Bront, wade in and take its attention. Roll.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 10] Ten. Longsword across the shoulder — [d8 → 3] three damage. Enough to make it mine.

**MAB:** It works. The bear roars — a sound you feel in your teeth — and rounds on Bront, forgetting the rogue in the water. Sable, crawl out, you're free. Quill, its back's to you now. Do something clever.

**QUILL:** Firebolt, and this time I aim for the dry fur on its back, not the wet. [d20 → 8] Eight — it lands! [d6 → 4] Four fire damage, and the fur catches, just a little, just enough to panic it. A bear does not understand fire. It understands "leave."

**FIFE:** And now Fife's music makes sense at last! I play the sound of a bigger, angrier bear — a rival! Begone, or face my imaginary champion! [d20 → 4] Four. The bear does not believe in the imaginary champion. But it's burned, and outnumbered, and the berries suddenly aren't worth it.

**MAB:** Sable, you're up and dripping and furious and it's turning to run. One parting shot?

**SABLE:** [d20 → 14] Fourteen. Dagger into its retreating backside. [d4 → 2] Two damage and a lot of insult. "THAT is for the river!"

**MAB:** The Brown Bear crashes off into the huckleberries and is gone, leaving broken branches and a trail of shed fur. You've bought the waymarker stone and the crossing. Sable is soaked to the bone and has now nearly died twice, for a running total of one copper.

**BRONT:** Told you. Birds know things.

## §3 Second Branch — "The Thing in the Wallow" (Giant Boar)

**MAB:** Past the stream the trail forks again, and the carved-face stones point you down the left branch, into deeper, older wood — trunks wider than doors, no birdsong now at all, just the drip of yesterday's rain. The ground turns soft, then sucking. You've walked into a wallow: a stinking pit of churned black mud, deep hoofprints, and a smell like a barnyard that gave up. Something big lives here. Marching order?

**QUILL:** Me in the middle, obviously. Fragile and expensive.

**MAB:** Noted. Fife's on point. Fife, Perception as you pick across the mud.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 12] Twelve. Fife detects... an excellent echo in this clearing. Truly, the acoustics are —

**MAB:** [d20 → 12] The acoustics carry a grunt back to you from directly behind a fallen log. The log explodes. A Giant Boar comes through it — not around it, THROUGH it — bark and splinters everywhere, tusks like curved knives, tiny furious eyes, forty-two hit points and building speed. It's already committed to a charge and Fife is the target. Fife, Dexterity save to not get gored.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 1] A ONE. Fife, patron of poets, has rolled a — no. NO.

**MAB:** Yes. Fife, you strike a heroic pose instead of moving, which is the single worst thing a person can do in front of a charging boar. It catches you full in the chest with its tusks and TOSSES you — [d8 → 5] five damage and you sail backward into the mud and land in the wallow with an undignified splat. Your rapier goes one way, your dignity another. You're prone, winded, and covered in filth.

**SABLE:** Best day of my life. Someone else is in the mud for once.

**BRONT:** Get up. Sing later.

**MAB:** The boar overshoots, skids in its own wallow, and has to turn — that's your window. Bront, Sable, hit it before it winds up again.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 17] Seventeen. Longsword down between the shoulders — [d8 → 3] three — and I set my feet to hold it.

**SABLE:** And I go for the eye. [d20 → 10] Ten, it's thrashing, hard to aim — [d4 → 3] three, just a jab, but it's bleeding now and it's ANGRY now.

**MAB:** It rounds on Bront, the one holding it, and gores. [d6 → 3] Three damage — Bront grunts, takes it on the hip, holds the line. Quill, it's pinned against Bront and it's not going anywhere. Take your time.

**QUILL:** Finally. A stationary target and dry fur. Firebolt, center of mass. [d20 → 15] Fifteen — [d6 → 5] five fire damage, and this time it catches proper. The boar screams.

**FIFE:** And FIFE rises from the muck! Filthy, magnificent, and RHYMING! From the wallow the bard finds his fallen rapier and drives it home for the finish — [d20 → 20] A NATURAL TWENTY, from the mud, from ROCK BOTTOM! Redemption!

**MAB:** From flat on your back in the filth, Fife, you snatch up the rapier and thrust up under the boar's jaw as it turns — crit. [d8 → 4] four, doubled, plus the fire, plus Bront's steel — the Giant Boar shudders, takes one more step, and drops in the wallow with a wet, final grunt. Fife, you are wearing an entire swamp, but you killed it. Roll for what's tangled in its tusks.

**FIFE:** [d6 → 3] Three... [d4 → 4] and four! Silver! Bits of old harness silver, worked into its tusks from some poor traveler — seven silver pieces, glinting in the mud!

**SABLE:** Give me that. Fife, you can't hold coins and gloat at the same time, it's a known fact.

## §4 Approach — "The Pack on the Ridge" (Gnoll Pack Lord)

**MAB:** Beyond the wallow the land rises. You climb a root-tangled ridge and the trees thin, and through them, at last — you see it. Down in a hollow, wrapped in a curtain of hanging moss, stands the shrine: a great carved stone face, mouth open, and something bright in that mouth catching the low green light. Gold. Real gold. But between you and it, on the ridgeline, someone has been busy. Bones. Cracked, gnawed, arranged into little totems. And the smell hits you — wet fur, carrion, laughter that isn't funny. Gnolls. Give me Perception, all together, quietly.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 18] Eighteen.

**MAB:** Eighteen sees them first, Bront — good. Three hyena-headed shapes crouched in the ferns, and behind them a fourth: bigger, a bone crown, a flail made of a spine. That's the Gnoll Pack Lord — forty-nine hit points, and worse, it makes the little ones brave. Its whole gift is turning cowards into a mob. You've got the drop on them. First strike is yours if you take it now.

**SABLE:** Sneak attack. My favorite kind of hello. [d20 → 13] Thirteen — dagger into the back of the nearest gnoll, quiet as you like. [d4 → 3] Three, plus the sneak — it drops without a sound. Three left.

**MAB:** Beautifully done. But the Pack Lord's head comes up — it smelled the blood — and it throws back its head and BAYS. That's the pack ability: the two remaining gnolls go from skittish to frenzied, foam and teeth, and they come at you both at once. Bront, brace.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 11] Eleven. Shield up. One bounces off. [d8 → 6] Wait — my swing back, six damage, opens its ribs.

**MAB:** The other gnoll gets past your guard and bites Fife. [d6 → 6] Six — a real one, Fife, teeth in the shoulder, you feel that. Fife, still muddy, still bleeding — how do you answer?

**FIFE:** Fife answers with ART. A shriek of the rapier and a verse so cutting it wounds the SOUL! [d20 → 9] Nine — the blade finds fur but not deep, [d8 → 5] five, and Fife's insult-song at least makes the beast hesitate. "You smell of a wet rug and BAD DECISIONS!"

**QUILL:** While they're bunched up — this is what area targeting is for. Firebolt into the pack, dry fur, close range. [d20 → 18] Eighteen! [d6 → 5] Five, and the second frenzied gnoll goes up like a torch and folds. That's the pack thinned to one — the Lord itself.

**MAB:** And the Pack Lord stops laughing. It steps over its dead and swings the spine-flail at Bront, the one it respects. [d8 → 3] Three damage — it rattles off the shield but drives Bront back a pace. This thing is strong. Bront, Second Wind — now's the time.

**BRONT:** [d8 → 4] Four. Back on my feet. Now the crown.

**MAB:** Everyone on the Lord. It's alone, it's cornered, and it knows it. Go.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 15] Fifteen — dagger under the arm — [d4 → 4] four!

**FIFE:** [d20 → 20] ANOTHER natural twenty for Fife! The bard is UNSTOPPABLE today! Rapier through the bone crown itself!

**MAB:** Crit, Fife — [d8 → 7] seven, doubled — the rapier punches through the Pack Lord's guard and it staggers, crown split, howling. It's not dead, but it's done — it turns and breaks for the trees, abandoning its own dead, the way its kind always does when the mob stops working. You hold the ridge. Below you the shrine waits, gold gleaming in its stone mouth. And the moss curtain around it... just moved. On its own. When there's no wind.

**QUILL:** There's no wind.

**SABLE:** There's never any good news in this forest, is there.

## §5 Boss — "The Lady in the Moss" (Yuan-ti Malison)

**MAB:** You come down off the ridge into the hollow. Up close the shrine is enormous — a carved stone face taller than Bront, worn smooth by rain, mouth gaping, and packed into that stone mouth: coins, cups, a crown, a fortune in old gold going green at the edges. It's everything the woodcutter promised. And draped across the offering, half-in and half-out of the hanging moss, is a woman. Beautiful, still, watching you with eyes that don't blink. Her voice is warm as honey.

**MAB (as the lady):** "Travelerssss. You came all thisss way. You must be so tired. Come — ressst. Take what you like." She smiles, and the smile has too many teeth, and below the waist the moss isn't moss at all. It's coils. Scales. She's a Yuan-ti Malison — snake from the ribs down — and she has been charming dinner into this hollow for a hundred years. Sixty-six hit points, spellcaster, and a stare that turns your own legs against you. Sable, you're closest to the gold. She's looking right at you. Wisdom save.

**SABLE:** [d20 → 18] Eighteen — I look at the floor. Nice try, snake. "How much is all that worth, anyway?"

**MAB:** She laughs, delighted you're immune, and drops the sweetness. "Then we do it the OLD way." She rears up out of the offering, and the whole hollow hisses — dozens of small snakes pouring out of the shrine's eyes and mouth at her command. Fife, one's already at your ankle. Save.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 1] ...Fife's luck has RUN OUT. A natural one.

**MAB:** After all that glory, Fife — the little serpent strikes and you feel the venom go in cold. [d8 → 7] Seven damage and you're poisoned — everything you roll for a while comes harder, your hands shaking, your grand verses coming out as a croak. The Malison points at Bront and hits him with a spell — [d6 → 4] four force damage, a lash of green light. This is the real fight. No more digging out. Hold together. Bront, you first.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 17] Seventeen. Longsword into the coils. [d8 → 8] Eight. She bleeds. Good.

**MAB:** She does bleed — black and hissing — and she does NOT like it. She whips her tail and knocks you sprawling across the offering, coins scattering. Quill, she's lit up in the open and you have been waiting all day for dry scales.

**QUILL:** All day. Firebolt, both hands, everything I've got. [d20 → 13] Thirteen — [d6 → 2] two, low roll, but it lands and it BURNS, and a snake does not like fire any more than a bear did. She flinches off Bront.

**SABLE:** My turn. While she's flinching. Sneak attack up the coils. [d20 → 2] Two — I slip on loose gold and nearly fall in the mouth of the shrine. Nothing.

**MAB:** She catches you by the collar, Sable, holds you over the stone mouth, and hisses in your face. Fife — poisoned, shaking — your friend's about to be dropped down a stone throat. Do something.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 9] Nine — through the poison, through the shaking, Fife lifts the rapier and pricks her tail, just enough — [d8 → 5] five — that she drops Sable to deal with him. "Unhand the rogue, serpent! She owes Fife SEVEN SILVER!"

**MAB:** She flings Sable aside and turns her full fury on the poisoned bard, spell crackling — [d6 → 3] three force, Fife, you're on your knees now, hanging on. She's hurt but she's winning on points. Bront, back on your feet — this needs to turn NOW.

**BRONT:** [d20 → 18] Eighteen. [d8 → 6] Six. Carving her down.

**MAB:** You open a long wound down her side and she screams — a real scream now, not honey, pain. She's below half. She coils back to strike Bront dead. Quill — set her up.

**QUILL:** Firebolt to the eyes. [d20 → 19] Nineteen — [d6 → 2] two damage but she's BLINDED, clawing at burnt eyes, her strike goes wide of Bront entirely. Sable — NOW, while she can't see you.

**SABLE:** Now she can't stare at me AND she can't see me. [d20 → 11] Eleven — dagger deep into the coils, [d4 → 4] four, twisting. "That's for hanging me over a hole!"

**MAB:** She's blind, bleeding, furious, wounded — and cornered against her own shrine. This is the moment. Whoever strikes true right now ends it. Fife — it should be you. You've earned it and you've suffered for it. Poisoned hands and all. Roll.

**FIFE:** [d20 → 20] ...A NATURAL TWENTY. Fife — poisoned, muddy, half-dead, third natural twenty of the day — FIFE ENDS THE LADY IN THE MOSS.

**MAB:** From your knees, Fife, you drive the rapier straight up as she strikes blind — a crit, the killing blow. [d8 → 6] six, doubled — the blade goes home under her heart and the Yuan-ti Malison shudders, her hundred little snakes going still all at once, and she folds back into the moss she came from and does not move again. The hollow goes quiet. And then — far off, faint — a single bird starts singing again. Then another. The wood lets its breath out.

**BRONT:** Good hit. Bard.

**FIFE:** *(faintly, from the ground)* Fife would like to be poisoned somewhere with a bed now, please.

**MAB:** Roll for the mouth of the shrine, someone with steady hands.

**QUILL:** [d20 → 18] Eighteen. [d6 → 6] and [d6 → 3] — I count it properly, because someone has to. [d4 → 1] The offering comes to two hundred and forty gold, a silver cup worth another fifty, and a small green stone that hums when you hold it — magic, unidentified, mine to study.

**SABLE:** Two hundred and forty GOLD. *(long pause)* I take back everything I said about the forest.

**BRONT:** You nearly died four times.

**SABLE:** For two hundred and forty gold I'd do it five. Help Fife up. We're rich, and I'm never coming back here.

**MAB:** You gather the gold, sling the poisoned bard between your shoulders, and climb back up out of the hollow the way you came — past the ridge of bones, the wallow, the stream, the silent web — and the birdsong follows you the whole way out, like the wood is glad to see the back of you. The Whispering Wood keeps its next secret to itself. For now. That's the dungeon. Well fought, all of you. Even you, Sable. Especially your one copper.
